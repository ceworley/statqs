We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 47.52 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{6.26}{\sqrt{34}} = 1.0735811\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 47.52 and a standard deviation of 1.0735811. \[\bar{X} \sim N(47.52,\,1.0735811) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{1.41}{1.0735811} = 1.3133614 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.31\) or \(z=1.32\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 1.3133614) = 0.8109388 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.31) = 0.8098 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.32) = 0.8132 \]
