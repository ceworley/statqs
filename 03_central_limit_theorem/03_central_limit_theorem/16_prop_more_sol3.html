We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.33\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.33)(0.67)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0361702 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2988-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= -0.8625883 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -0.8625883) ~=~ 0.8058181 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -0.87) ~=~ 0.8078 \] \[P(Z > -0.86) ~=~ 0.8051 \]
