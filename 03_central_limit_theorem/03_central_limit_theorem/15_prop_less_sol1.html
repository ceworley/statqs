We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.24\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.24)(0.76)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0359669 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2021-0.24}{0.0359669} \\\\ &= -1.053747 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -1.053747) ~=~ 0.1459994 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -1.06) ~=~ 0.1446 \] \[P(Z < -1.05) ~=~ 0.1469 \]
