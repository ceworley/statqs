We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (114)(9.96) ~=~ 1135.44 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.75)\sqrt{114} = 18.6848869\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 1135.44 and a standard deviation of 18.6848869. \[\sum{X} \sim N(1135.44,\,18.6848869) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{24.56}{18.6848869} = 1.3144313 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.31\) or \(z=1.32\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 1.3144313) = 0.1887011 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.31) = 0.1902 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.32) = 0.1868 \]
