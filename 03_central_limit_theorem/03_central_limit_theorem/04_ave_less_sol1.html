We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 53.6 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{9.03}{\sqrt{865}} = 0.3070292\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 53.6 and a standard deviation of 0.3070292. \[\bar{X} \sim N(53.6,\,0.3070292) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{53.66-53.6}{0.3070292} = 0.1954211 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.19\) or \(z=0.2\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z < 0.1954211) = 0.5774684 \] \[P(Z < 0.19) = 0.5753 \] \[P(Z < 0.2) = 0.5793 \]
