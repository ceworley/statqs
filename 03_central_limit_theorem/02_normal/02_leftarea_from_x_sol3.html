First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(M\) axis by adding integer multiples of 40 to 270. We know to shade toward the left because small values of \(M\) satisfy the condition \(M<320\).


We are given a specific \(m\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[m = 320 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{m-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{320-270}{40} \\\\ &= 1.25 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(M<320) ~=~ P(Z<1.25) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z<1.25)\). To do this, you just need a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
1.23 0.8907
1.24 0.8925
1.25 0.8944
1.26 0.8962
1.27 0.898

Thus, we find our answer. \[P(M<320) = 0.8944 \]
