Start with a sketch. The total two-tail area is 0.6, so each tail has half that area.


Method 1: Determine the area of each tail and the center. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, \[P(Z>z) = \frac{0.6}{2} = 0.3\]

\[P(Z<-z) = \frac{0.6}{2} = 0.3\]

\[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<z) &= 1-P(|Z|>z) \\ &= 1-0.6 \\ &= 0.4 \end{aligned}\]

Find the left area.

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.3+0.4 \\ &= 0.7 \end{aligned}\]

You should go to your \(z\)-table and find the \(z\)-score with the left area closest to 0.7.

It turns out the exact answer is \(z=0.5244005\), which could be found by using an inverse normal function. On a spreadsheet:

=Norm.Inv(0.7,0,1)

Using R:

twotailarea = 0.6
onetailarea = twotailarea/2
centralarea = 1-twotailarea
leftarea = onetailarea + centralarea
qnorm( leftarea )

Method 2: Another way to get 0.7 is by subtracting half of 0.6 from 1. \[P(Z<z) ~=~ 1-\frac{0.6}{2}~=~ 1-0.3 ~=~ 0.7\]


Then, use the table. Or, R:

twotailarea = 0.6
leftarea = 1 - twotailarea/2
qnorm( leftarea )
