x = c(388, 387, 368, 326, 339, 353, 359, 411, 376, 342, 334, 353, 353, 350, 365, 340, 369, 332, 354, 367, 362, 377, 358, 370, 383, 344, 331, 360, 354, 348, 350, 345, 375, 384, 381, 350, 386, 353, 397, 371, 349)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.96   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=41\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=360.83\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=19.26\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{19.26}{\sqrt{41}}=3.01\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=41\), then \(\text{df}=40\), and \(P(|T|<2.12)=0.96\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=2.12\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(2.12)(19.26)}{\sqrt{41}}=6.38\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=360.83-6.38=354.45\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=360.83+6.38=367.21\).
