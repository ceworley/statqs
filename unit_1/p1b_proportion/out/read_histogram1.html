    A random procedure generated 100 measurements, which were organized into the histogram shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

    4. Evaluate prop[|ğ±âˆ’35|>3]\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-35|>3].

    Solution

    You may find it helpful to convert the densities to frequencies by multiplying each density by both the total sample size (n=100n=100) and the width of the bar (2).


    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 39 measurements less than 34. So, prop[ğ±<34]=0.39\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<34]=0.39.
    2. There are 14 measurements more than 38. So, prop[ğ±>38]=0.14\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>38]=0.14.
    3. There are 22 measurements closer than 1 units from 33. So, prop[|ğ±âˆ’33|<1]=0.22\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-33|<1]=0.22.
    4. There are 31 measurements further than 3 units from 35. So, prop[|ğ±âˆ’35|>3]=0.31\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-35|>3]=0.31.
    5. A boundary at b=36b=36 has 63 measurements less than it. So, prop[ğ±<36]=0.63\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<36]=0.63.
    6. A boundary at b=32b=32 has 83 measurements more than it. So, prop[ğ±>32]=0.83\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>32]=0.83.
    7. An interval with radius r=3r=3 around center 33 contains 58 measurements. So, prop[|ğ±âˆ’33|<3]=0.58\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-33|<3]=0.58.
    8. An interval with radius r=2r=2 around center 38 excludes 66 measurements. So, prop[|ğ±âˆ’38|>2]=0.66\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-38|>2]=0.66.
