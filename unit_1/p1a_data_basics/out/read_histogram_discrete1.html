    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 44 measurements less than 3.5. So, #[ğ±<3.5]=44\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=44.
    2. There are 45 measurements more than 2.5. So, #[ğ±>2.5]=45\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=45.
    3. There are 9 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 3. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’3|<0.5]=9\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=9.
    4. There are 64 measurements further than 0.5 units from 2. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’2|>0.5]=64\#[|\mathbf{x}-2|>0.5]=64.
    5. A boundary at b=4.5b=4.5 has 51 measurements less than it. So, #[ğ±<4.5]=51\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=51.
    6. A boundary at b=3.5b=3.5 has 36 measurements more than it. So, #[ğ±>3.5]=36\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=36.
    7. An interval with radius r=0.5r=0.5 around center 3 contains 9 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’3|<0.5]=9\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=9.
    8. An interval with radius r=1.5r=1.5 around center 3 excludes 48 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’3|>1.5]=48\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=48.
