  1. Question

    A random procedure generated 60 measurements, which were organized into the histogram shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).



    1. Evaluate #[ğ±<73]\#[\mathbf{x}<73].
    2. Evaluate #[ğ±>75]\#[\mathbf{x}>75].
    3. Evaluate #[|ğ±âˆ’75|<2]\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|<2].
    4. Evaluate #[|ğ±âˆ’74.5|>2.5]\#[|\mathbf{x}-74.5|>2.5].
    5. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[ğ±<b]=59\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 59.
    6. Evaluate boundary bb such that #[ğ±>b]=16\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 16.
    7. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|ğ±âˆ’73.5|<r]=7\#[|\mathbf{x}-73.5|<r] = 7.
    8. Evaluate radius rr such that #[|ğ±âˆ’76.5|>r]=60\#[|\mathbf{x}-76.5|>r] = 60.

    Solution

    The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


    1. There are 44 measurements less than 73. So, #[ğ±<73]=44\#[\mathbf{x}<73]=44.
    2. There are 3 measurements more than 75. So, #[ğ±>75]=3\#[\mathbf{x}>75]=3.
    3. There are 15 measurements closer than 2 units from 75. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’75|<2]=15\#[|\mathbf{x}-75|<2]=15.
    4. There are 39 measurements further than 2.5 units from 74.5. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’74.5|>2.5]=39\#[|\mathbf{x}-74.5|>2.5]=39.
    5. A boundary at b=76b=76 has 59 measurements less than it. So, #[ğ±<76]=59\#[\mathbf{x}<76]=59.
    6. A boundary at b=73b=73 has 16 measurements more than it. So, #[ğ±>73]=16\#[\mathbf{x}>73]=16.
    7. An interval with radius r=0.5r=0.5 around center 73.5 contains 7 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’73.5|<0.5]=7\#[|\mathbf{x}-73.5|<0.5]=7.
    8. An interval with radius r=0.5r=0.5 around center 76.5 excludes 60 measurements. So, #[|ğ±âˆ’76.5|>0.5]=60\#[|\mathbf{x}-76.5|>0.5]=60.
