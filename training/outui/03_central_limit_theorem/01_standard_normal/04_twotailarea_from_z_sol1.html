First, you need to identify that we are looking for a two-tail area (the sum of the left and right tails). This is because \(z\)-scores far from 0 satisfy \(|Z|>0.93\), while \(z\)-scores near 0 do not satisfy the inequality.

Start with a sketch.


Remember, the entire area is always 1. Most z-tables only provides left areas, so three methods are shown to get the two-tail area from a left-area (cumulative probability) table.

Method 1: First find the area of the left tail. \[P(Z<-0.93) = 0.1762\] Recognize normal distributions are symmetric, so we also know the area of the right tail. \[P(Z>0.93) = 0.1762 \] The two areas add to our desired two-tail area. \[\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|>0.93\right) &= P(Z<-0.93)+P(Z>0.93) \\ &= 0.1762 + 0.1762 \\ &= 0.3524 \end{aligned}\]

This technique can be summarized with the following formula: \[P\left(|Z|>z\right) = 2\cdot P(Z<-z)\] assuming \(z>0\).

So, in our case (when \(z=0.93\)), \[\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|<0.93\right) &= 2\cdot P(Z<-0.93) \\ &= 2\cdot0.1762 \\ &= 0.3524 \end{aligned}\]

This method is shown graphically:


Notice we need to add both tails.

Method 2: You can achieve the same result by using the following formula: \[P\left(|Z|>z\right) = 2\cdot\left[1-P(Z<z) \right] \] So, \[\begin{aligned} P\left(|Z|>0.93\right) &= 2\cdot\left[1-P(Z<0.93) \right] \\ &= 2\cdot\left[1-0.8238\right] \\ &= 2\cdot0.1762\\ &= 0.3524 \end{aligned}\]

It might be helpful to visualize with a spinner.

=2*NORM.DIST(-0.93,0,1,TRUE)

In R, you could use the pnorm function.

2*pnorm(-0.93)
