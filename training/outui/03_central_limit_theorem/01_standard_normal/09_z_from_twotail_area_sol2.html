Start with a sketch. The total two-tail area is 0.7, so each tail has half that area.


Method 1: Determine the area of each tail and the center. Both tails have the same area, and all three areas add to 1. Thus, \[P(Z>z) = \frac{0.7}{2} = 0.35\]

\[P(Z<-z) = \frac{0.7}{2} = 0.35\]

\[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<z) &= 1-P(|Z|>z) \\ &= 1-0.7 \\ &= 0.3 \end{aligned}\]

Find the left area.

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= 0.35+0.3 \\ &= 0.65 \end{aligned}\]
