We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.08\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.08)(0.92)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0217208 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.0737-0.08}{0.0217208} \\\\ &= -0.290044 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.290044) ~=~ 0.3858913 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.3) ~=~ 0.3821 \] \[P(Z < -0.29) ~=~ 0.3859 \]
