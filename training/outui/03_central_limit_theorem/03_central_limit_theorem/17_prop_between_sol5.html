We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.27\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.27)(0.73)}{45}} ~=~ 0.0661816 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2333-0.27}{0.0661816} \\\\ &= -0.5540314 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2556-0.27}{0.0661816} \\\\ &= -0.2182548 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.5540314 < Z < -0.2182548) ~=~ 0.1238366 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.56 < Z < -0.22) ~=~ 0.1252 \] \[P(-0.55 < Z < -0.21) ~=~ 0.1256 \] \[P(-0.56 < Z < -0.21) ~=~ 0.1217 \] \[P(-0.55 < Z < -0.22) ~=~ 0.1291 \]
