We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (76)(85.54) ~=~ 6501.04 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (4.53)\sqrt{76} = 39.4916244\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 6501.04 and a standard deviation of 39.4916244. \[\sum{X} \sim N(6501.04,\,39.4916244) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{38.96}{39.4916244} = 0.9865383 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.98\) or \(z=0.99\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.9865383) = 0.323869 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.98) = 0.327 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.99) = 0.3222 \]
