We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (100)(0.87) ~=~ 87\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(100)(0.87)(0.13)} ~=~ 3.3630343 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{87.5-87}{3.3630343} \\\\ &= 0.1486753 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{89.5-87}{3.3630343} \\\\ &= 0.7433763 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(0.1486753 < Z < 0.7433763) ~=~ 0.212278 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(0.14 < Z < 0.74) ~=~ 0.2147 \] \[P(0.15 < Z < 0.75) ~=~ 0.2138 \] \[P(0.14 < Z < 0.75) ~=~ 0.2108 \] \[P(0.15 < Z < 0.74) ~=~ 0.2177 \]
