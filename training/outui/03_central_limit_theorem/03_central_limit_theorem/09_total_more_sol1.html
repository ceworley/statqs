We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (150)(87.87) ~=~ 13180.5 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (5.22)\sqrt{150} = 63.9316823\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 13180.5 and a standard deviation of 63.9316823. \[\sum{X} \sim N(13180.5,\,63.9316823) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{13200-13180.5}{63.9316823} = 0.3050131 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.3\) or \(z=0.31\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 0.3050131) = 0.3801781 \] \[P(Z > 0.3) = 0.3821 \] \[P(Z > 0.31) = 0.3783 \]
