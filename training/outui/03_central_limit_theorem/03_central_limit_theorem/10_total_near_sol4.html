We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (48)(65.47) ~=~ 3142.56 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.36)\sqrt{48} = 9.4223564\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 3142.56 and a standard deviation of 9.4223564. \[\sum{X} \sim N(3142.56,\,9.4223564) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{7.44}{9.4223564} = 0.7896114 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.78\) or \(z=0.79\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.7896114) = 0.5702453 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.78) = 0.5646 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.79) = 0.5704 \]
