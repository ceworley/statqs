We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (57)(9.8) ~=~ 558.6 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.98)\sqrt{57} = 14.9486722\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 558.6 and a standard deviation of 14.9486722. \[\sum{X} \sim N(558.6,\,14.9486722) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{545-558.6}{14.9486722} = -0.9097798 \]

You can round that to \(z=-0.91\) or \(z=-0.9\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -0.9097798) = 0.8185307 \] \[P(Z > -0.91) = 0.8186 \] \[P(Z > -0.9) = 0.8159 \]
