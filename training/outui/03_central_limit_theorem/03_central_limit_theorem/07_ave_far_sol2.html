We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 53.02 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{10.37}{\sqrt{64}} = 1.29625\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 53.02 and a standard deviation of 1.29625. \[\bar{X} \sim N(53.02,\,1.29625) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{2.72}{1.29625} = 2.0983607 \]

You can round that to \(z=2.09\) or \(z=2.1\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 2.0983607) = 0.0358733 \] \[P(|Z| > 2.09) = 0.0366 \] \[P(|Z| > 2.1) = 0.0358 \]
