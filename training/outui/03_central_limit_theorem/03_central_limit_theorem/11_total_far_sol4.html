We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (70)(68.88) ~=~ 4821.6 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (17.02)\sqrt{70} = 142.3995365\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 4821.6 and a standard deviation of 142.3995365. \[\sum{X} \sim N(4821.6,\,142.3995365) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{88.4}{142.3995365} = 0.6207885 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.62\) or \(z=0.63\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.6207885) = 0.5347388 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.62) = 0.5352 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.63) = 0.5286 \]
