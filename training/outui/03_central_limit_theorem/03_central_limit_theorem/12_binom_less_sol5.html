We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (124)(0.09) ~=~ 11.16\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(124)(0.09)(0.91)} ~=~ 3.1867852 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{9.5-11.16}{3.1867852} \\\\ &= -0.5209011 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.5209011) ~=~ 0.3012178 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.53) ~=~ 0.2981 \] \[P(Z < -0.52) ~=~ 0.3015 \]
