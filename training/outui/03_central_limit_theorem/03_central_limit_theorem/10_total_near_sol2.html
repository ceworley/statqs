We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (108)(88.37) ~=~ 9543.96 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (3)\sqrt{108} = 31.1769145\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 9543.96 and a standard deviation of 31.1769145. \[\sum{X} \sim N(9543.96,\,31.1769145) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{6.04}{31.1769145} = 0.1937331 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.19\) or \(z=0.2\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.1937331) = 0.1536151 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.19) = 0.1506 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.2) = 0.1586 \]
