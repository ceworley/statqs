We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (100)(10.5) \\ &= 1050 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (5.7662813)\sqrt{100} \\ &= 57.662813 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(1050,\,57.662813) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 100 rolls of 20-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{1099.5-1050}{57.662813} ~=~ 0.8584389\]

You can round this to either 0.85 or 0.86. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 1099.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.8584389) \] \[P(Z<0.8584389) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<0.8584389) ~=~ 0.8046749\]

\[P(Z<0.85) ~=~ 0.8023\] \[P(Z<0.86) ~=~ 0.8051\]
