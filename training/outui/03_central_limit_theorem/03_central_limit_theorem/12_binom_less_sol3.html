We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (114)(0.8) ~=~ 91.2\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(114)(0.8)(0.2)} ~=~ 4.2708313 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{85.5-91.2}{4.2708313} \\\\ &= -1.3346348 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -1.3346348) ~=~ 0.090998 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -1.34) ~=~ 0.0901 \] \[P(Z < -1.33) ~=~ 0.0918 \]
