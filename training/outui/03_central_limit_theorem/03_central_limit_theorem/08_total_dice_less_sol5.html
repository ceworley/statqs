We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (225)(6.5) \\ &= 1462.5 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (3.4520525)\sqrt{225} \\ &= 51.7807879 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(1462.5,\,51.7807879) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 225 rolls of 12-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{1463.5-1462.5}{51.7807879} ~=~ 0.0193122\]

You can round this to either 0.01 or 0.02. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 1463.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.0193122) \] \[P(Z<0.0193122) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<0.0193122) ~=~ 0.507704\]

\[P(Z<0.01) ~=~ 0.504\] \[P(Z<0.02) ~=~ 0.508\]
