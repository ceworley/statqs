We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (166)(0.72) ~=~ 119.52\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(166)(0.72)(0.28)} ~=~ 5.784946 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{110.5-119.52}{5.784946} \\\\ &= -1.5592194 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -1.5592194) ~=~ 0.0594722 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -1.56) ~=~ 0.0594 \] \[P(Z < -1.55) ~=~ 0.0606 \]
