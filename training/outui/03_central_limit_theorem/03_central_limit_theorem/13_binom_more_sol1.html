We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (36)(0.48) ~=~ 17.28\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(36)(0.48)(0.52)} ~=~ 2.997599 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{21.5-17.28}{2.997599} \\\\ &= 1.4077934 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 1.4077934) ~=~ 0.0795961 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 1.4) ~=~ 0.0808 \] \[P(Z > 1.41) ~=~ 0.0793 \]
