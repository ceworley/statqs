We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (195)(44.12) ~=~ 8603.4 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (2.27)\sqrt{195} = 31.6988249\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 8603.4 and a standard deviation of 31.6988249. \[\sum{X} \sim N(8603.4,\,31.6988249) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{8660-8603.4}{31.6988249} = 1.7855551 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.78\) or \(z=1.79\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > 1.7855551) = 0.0370857 \] \[P(Z > 1.78) = 0.0375 \] \[P(Z > 1.79) = 0.0367 \]
