We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (100)(4.5) \\ &= 450 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (2.2912878)\sqrt{100} \\ &= 22.9128785 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(450,\,22.9128785) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 100 rolls of 8-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{412.5-450}{22.9128785} ~=~ -1.6366342\]

You can round this to either -1.64 or -1.63. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 412.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<-1.6366342) \] \[P(Z<-1.6366342) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<-1.6366342) ~=~ 0.0508535\]

\[P(Z<-1.64) ~=~ 0.0505\] \[P(Z<-1.63) ~=~ 0.0516\]
