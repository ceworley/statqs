We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.52\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.52)(0.48)}{48}} ~=~ 0.072111 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5104-0.52}{0.072111} \\\\ &= -0.1328969 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5521-0.52}{0.072111} \\\\ &= 0.4449158 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.1328969 < Z < 0.4449158) ~=~ 0.2246722 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.14 < Z < 0.44) ~=~ 0.2257 \] \[P(-0.13 < Z < 0.45) ~=~ 0.2253 \] \[P(-0.14 < Z < 0.45) ~=~ 0.2217 \] \[P(-0.13 < Z < 0.44) ~=~ 0.2293 \]
