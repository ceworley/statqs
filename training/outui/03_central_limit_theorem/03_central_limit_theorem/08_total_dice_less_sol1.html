We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (169)(6.5) \\ &= 1098.5 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (3.4520525)\sqrt{169} \\ &= 44.8766829 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(1098.5,\,44.8766829) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 169 rolls of 12-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{1122.5-1098.5}{44.8766829} ~=~ 0.5347989\]

You can round this to either 0.53 or 0.54. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 1122.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<0.5347989) \] \[P(Z<0.5347989) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<0.5347989) ~=~ 0.7036055\]

\[P(Z<0.53) ~=~ 0.7019\] \[P(Z<0.54) ~=~ 0.7054\]
