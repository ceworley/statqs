We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (35)(50.79) ~=~ 1777.65 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (4.13)\sqrt{35} = 24.4334095\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 1777.65 and a standard deviation of 24.4334095. \[\sum{X} \sim N(1777.65,\,24.4334095) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{12.35}{24.4334095} = 0.5054555 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.5\) or \(z=0.51\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 0.5054555) = 0.386761 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.5) = 0.383 \] \[P(|Z| < 0.51) = 0.39 \]
