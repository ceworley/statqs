We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (192)(0.85) ~=~ 163.2\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(192)(0.85)(0.15)} ~=~ 4.9477268 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{156.5-163.2}{4.9477268} \\\\ &= -1.3541572 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -1.3541572) ~=~ 0.9121569 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -1.36) ~=~ 0.9131 \] \[P(Z > -1.35) ~=~ 0.9115 \]
