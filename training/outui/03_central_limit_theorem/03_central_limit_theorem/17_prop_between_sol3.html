We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.4\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.4)(0.6)}{58}} ~=~ 0.0643268 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4052-0.4}{0.0643268} \\\\ &= 0.0804084 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4914-0.4}{0.0643268} \\\\ &= 1.4205491 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(0.0804084 < Z < 1.4205491) ~=~ 0.3902323 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(0.08 < Z < 1.42) ~=~ 0.3903 \] \[P(0.09 < Z < 1.43) ~=~ 0.3877 \] \[P(0.08 < Z < 1.43) ~=~ 0.3863 \] \[P(0.09 < Z < 1.42) ~=~ 0.3917 \]
