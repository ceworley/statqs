We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 50.62 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{10.02}{\sqrt{34}} = 1.7184158\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 50.62 and a standard deviation of 1.7184158. \[\bar{X} \sim N(50.62,\,1.7184158) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{3.42}{1.7184158} = 1.9902051 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.99\) or \(z=2\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 1.9902051) = 0.9534317 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.99) = 0.9534 \] \[P(|Z| < 2) = 0.9544 \]
