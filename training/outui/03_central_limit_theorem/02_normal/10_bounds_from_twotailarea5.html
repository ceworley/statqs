Random variable \(C\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.15\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.04\). \[C \sim \text{N}(0.15,\,0.04) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|C-0.15\big|>d\right) = 0.28\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 28% of the time.
  2. Let \(c_1 = 0.15-d\), such that \(P\left(C < c_1\right) = \frac{0.28}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_1\).
  3. Let \(c_2 = 0.15+d\), such that \(P\left(C > c_2\right) = \frac{0.28}{2}\). Evaluate \(c_2\).
