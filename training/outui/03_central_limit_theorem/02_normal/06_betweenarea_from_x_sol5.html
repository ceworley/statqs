First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(M\) axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 260. We know to shade the between \(180\) and \(390\) because those values satisfy the condition \(180 < M < 390\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{m_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{180-260}{50} \\\\ &= -1.6 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{m_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{390-260}{50} \\\\ &= 2.6 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(180 < M < 390\right) ~=~ P(-1.6<Z<2.6)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-1.6<Z<2.6)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1.6<Z<2.6) &= P(Z<2.6)-P(Z<-1.6)\\ &= 0.9953-0.0548 \\ &= 0.9405 \end{aligned}\]


ANSWERS: 0.9405
TOLERANCES: 0.001
