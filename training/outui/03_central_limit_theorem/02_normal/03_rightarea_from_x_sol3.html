First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(K\) axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 480. We know to shade toward the right because large values of \(K\) satisfy the condition \(K>420\).


We are given a specific \(k\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[k = 420 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{k-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{420-480}{50} \\\\ &= -1.2 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(K>420) ~=~ P(Z>-1.2) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z>-1.2)\).

To do this, you need to remember that right-area events are complementary to left-area events. \[P(Z>-1.2) ~=~ 1-P(Z<-1.2) \]

You can use a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
-1.22 0.1112
-1.21 0.1131
-1.2 0.1151
-1.19 0.117
-1.18 0.119

Thus, we find our answer. \[\begin{aligned} P(K>420) &= 1-0.1151 \\ &= 0.8849 \end{aligned}\]
