Random variable \(X\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 7.3\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 2\). \[X \sim \text{N}(7.3,\,2) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|X-7.3\big|>d\right) = 0.66\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 66% of the time.
  2. Let \(x_1 = 7.3-d\), such that \(P\left(X < x_1\right) = \frac{0.66}{2}\). Evaluate \(x_1\).
  3. Let \(x_2 = 7.3+d\), such that \(P\left(X > x_2\right) = \frac{0.66}{2}\). Evaluate \(x_2\).
