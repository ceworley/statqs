First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(K\) axis by adding integer multiples of 80 to 940. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 940 satisfy the condition \(\big|K-940\big| > 60\). We draw the boundaries at \(940-60=880\) and \(940+60=1000\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|K-940\big| = 60\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|K-940\big| > 60\right) ~=~ P\big(K<880 \text{ OR } K>1000\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{880-940}{80} \\\\ &= \frac{-60}{80} \\\\ &= -0.75 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{1000-940}{80} \\\\ &= \frac{60}{80} \\\\ &= 0.75 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|K-940\big| > 60\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.75\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.75)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{60}{80} = 0.75\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.75) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.75) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.7734 \\ &= 0.4532 \end{aligned}\]
