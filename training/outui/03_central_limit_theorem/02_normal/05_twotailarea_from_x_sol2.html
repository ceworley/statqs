First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(R\) axis by adding integer multiples of 15 to 64. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 64 satisfy the condition \(\big|R-64\big| > 3\). We draw the boundaries at \(64-3=61\) and \(64+3=67\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|R-64\big| = 3\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|R-64\big| > 3\right) ~=~ P\big(R<61 \text{ OR } R>67\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{61-64}{15} \\\\ &= \frac{-3}{15} \\\\ &= -0.2 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{67-64}{15} \\\\ &= \frac{3}{15} \\\\ &= 0.2 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|R-64\big| > 3\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.2\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.2)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{3}{15} = 0.2\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.2) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.2) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.5793 \\ &= 0.8414 \end{aligned}\]
