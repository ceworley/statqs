First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(M\) axis by adding integer multiples of 50 to 160. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 160 satisfy the condition \(\big|M-160\big| > 30\). We draw the boundaries at \(160-30=130\) and \(160+30=190\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|M-160\big| = 30\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|M-160\big| > 30\right) ~=~ P\big(M<130 \text{ OR } M>190\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{130-160}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{-30}{50} \\\\ &= -0.6 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{190-160}{50} \\\\ &= \frac{30}{50} \\\\ &= 0.6 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|M-160\big| > 30\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.6\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.6)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{30}{50} = 0.6\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.6) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.6) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.7257 \\ &= 0.5486 \end{aligned}\]
