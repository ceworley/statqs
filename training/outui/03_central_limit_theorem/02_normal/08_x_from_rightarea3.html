Random variable \(B\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 49\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 16\). \[B \sim \text{N}(49,\,16) \] Evaluate \(b\) such that \(P(B>b) = 0.9\). In other words, determine an lower boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 49 and standard deviation 16 lands over that boundary 90% of the time.
