Random variable \(B\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 66\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 14\). \[B \sim \text{N}(66,\,14) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|B-66\big|>d\right) = 0.08\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands outside those boundaries 8% of the time.
  2. Let \(b_1 = 66-d\), such that \(P\left(B < b_1\right) = \frac{0.08}{2}\). Evaluate \(b_1\).
  3. Let \(b_2 = 66+d\), such that \(P\left(B > b_2\right) = \frac{0.08}{2}\). Evaluate \(b_2\).
