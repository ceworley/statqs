First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(R\) axis by adding integer multiples of 10 to 75. We know to shade the between \(72\) and \(81\) because those values satisfy the condition \(72 < R < 81\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{r_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{72-75}{10} \\\\ &= -0.3 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{r_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{81-75}{10} \\\\ &= 0.6 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(72 < R < 81\right) ~=~ P(-0.3<Z<0.6)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-0.3<Z<0.6)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-0.3<Z<0.6) &= P(Z<0.6)-P(Z<-0.3)\\ &= 0.7257-0.3821 \\ &= 0.3436 \end{aligned}\]
