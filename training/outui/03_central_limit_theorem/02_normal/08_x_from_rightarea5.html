Random variable \(D\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 5.9\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 1.5\). \[D \sim \text{N}(5.9,\,1.5) \] Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P(D>d) = 0.59\). In other words, determine an lower boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 5.9 and standard deviation 1.5 lands on a value more than that boundary 59% of the time.
