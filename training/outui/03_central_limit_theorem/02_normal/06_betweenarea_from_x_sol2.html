First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(I\) axis by adding integer multiples of 3 to 52. We know to shade the between \(46\) and \(49\) because those values satisfy the condition \(46 < I < 49\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{i_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{46-52}{3} \\\\ &= -2 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{i_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{49-52}{3} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(46 < I < 49\right) ~=~ P(-2<Z<-1)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-2<Z<-1)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-2<Z<-1) &= P(Z<-1)-P(Z<-2)\\ &= 0.1587-0.0228 \\ &= 0.1359 \end{aligned}\]
