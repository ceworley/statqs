First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(E\) axis by adding integer multiples of 2 to 15. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 15 satisfy the condition \(\big|E-15\big| > 1\). We draw the boundaries at \(15-1=14\) and \(15+1=16\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|E-15\big| = 1\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|E-15\big| > 1\right) ~=~ P\big(E<14 \text{ OR } E>16\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{14-15}{2} \\\\ &= \frac{-1}{2} \\\\ &= -0.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{16-15}{2} \\\\ &= \frac{1}{2} \\\\ &= 0.5 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|E-15\big| > 1\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>0.5\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>0.5)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{1}{2} = 0.5\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>0.5) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<0.5) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.6915 \\ &= 0.617 \end{aligned}\]
