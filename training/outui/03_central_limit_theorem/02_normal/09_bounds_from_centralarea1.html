Random variable \(C\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 9.6\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 3.2\). \[C \sim \text{N}(9.6,\,3.2) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|C-9.6\big|<d\right) = 0.82\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands between those boundaries 82% of the time.
  2. Let \(c_1 = 9.6-d\) and \(c_2 = 9.6+d\), such that \(P\left(c_1 < C < c_2\right) = 0.82\). Evaluate \(c_1\).
  3. Using the definitions above, evaluate \(c_2\)
