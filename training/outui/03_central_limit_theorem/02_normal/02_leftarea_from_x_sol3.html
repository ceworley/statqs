First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(Q\) axis by adding integer multiples of 0.08 to 0.46. We know to shade toward the left because small values of \(Q\) satisfy the condition \(Q<0.52\).


We are given a specific \(q\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[q = 0.52 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{q-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.52-0.46}{0.08} \\\\ &= 0.75 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(Q<0.52) ~=~ P(Z<0.75) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z<0.75)\). To do this, you just need a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
0.73 0.7673
0.74 0.7704
0.75 0.7734
0.76 0.7764
0.77 0.7794

Thus, we find our answer. \[P(Q<0.52) = 0.7734 \]
