First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(K\) axis by adding integer multiples of 5 to 89. We know to shade the between \(81\) and \(88\) because those values satisfy the condition \(81 < K < 88\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{k_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{81-89}{5} \\\\ &= -1.6 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{k_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{88-89}{5} \\\\ &= -0.2 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(81 < K < 88\right) ~=~ P(-1.6<Z<-0.2)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-1.6<Z<-0.2)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1.6<Z<-0.2) &= P(Z<-0.2)-P(Z<-1.6)\\ &= 0.4207-0.0548 \\ &= 0.3659 \end{aligned}\]
