First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(K\) axis by adding integer multiples of 1.8 to 7.4. We know to shade the the outsides because values far from 7.4 satisfy the condition \(\big|K-7.4\big|>d\) (regardless of the exact value of \(d\)). We don’t know exactly where to place the boundaries, but we know the two-tail area is 0.44.


As an intermediate step, let’s find \(z\) such that \(P(|Z|>z)=0.44\). First, we need to evaluate \(P(Z<z)\).

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= \frac{2-P(|Z|>z)}{2} \\ \\ &= \frac{2-0.44}{2} \\ \\ &= 0.78 \end{aligned}\]

You could have also drawn some pictures… we know there is symmetry and all the areas should add to 1.



We find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.78\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)

Either 0.77 or 0.78 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=0.7722\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(d\) score. \[\begin{aligned} d &= z\sigma \\ &= (0.7722)(1.8) \\ &= 1.39 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.



  1. We determined \(d=1.39\)
  2. It is easy to find \(k_1 = 7.4-1.39 = 6.01\)
  3. It is easy to find \(k_2 = 7.4+1.39 = 8.79\)
