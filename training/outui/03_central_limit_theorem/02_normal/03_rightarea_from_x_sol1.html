First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(E\) axis by adding integer multiples of 90 to 350. We know to shade toward the right because large values of \(E\) satisfy the condition \(E>440\).


We are given a specific \(e\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[e = 440 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{e-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{440-350}{90} \\\\ &= 1 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(E>440) ~=~ P(Z>1) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z>1)\).

To do this, you need to remember that right-area events are complementary to left-area events. \[P(Z>1) ~=~ 1-P(Z<1) \]

You can use a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
0.98 0.8365
0.99 0.8389
1 0.8413
1.01 0.8438
1.02 0.8461

Thus, we find our answer. \[\begin{aligned} P(E>440) &= 1-0.8413 \\ &= 0.1587 \end{aligned}\]
