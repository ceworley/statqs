01_data_basics/02_proportion/01_list_proportion_basic_v3

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 11, 19, 10, 19, 13, 14, 17, 18, 16, 10 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_proportion_basic_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are less than 13? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<13]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are less than or equal to 19? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}\le19]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are greater than 14? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>14]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are greater than or equal to 16? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}\ge16]\).
  5. If the proportion of measurements less than \(x_{\text{c}}\) is 0.8, and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 0.8\).
  6. If the proportion of measurements less than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\) is 0.2, and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 0.2\).
  7. If the proportion of measurements greater than \(x_{\text{c}}\) is 0.2, and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 0.2\).
  8. If the proportion of measurements greater than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\) is 0.8, and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 0.8\).
