01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v4

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 83, 81, 83, 82, 86, 83, 85, 82, 87, 85 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 82.5 cm to 84.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>82.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<84.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[82.5<\mathbf{x}<84.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 82.5 cm to 83.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<82.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>83.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(82.5<\mathbf{x}<83.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 3.5 cm from 84 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84\big|<3.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1 cm from 84.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 84.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-84.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.2\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 85.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-85.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.3\).
