01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v5

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 54, 59, 55, 51, 56, 53, 52, 55, 59, 58 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 50.5 cm to 54.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>50.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<54.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[50.5<\mathbf{x}<54.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 56.5 cm to 59.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<56.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>59.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(56.5<\mathbf{x}<59.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 0.5 cm from 54 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|<0.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 0.5 cm from 53 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-53\big|>0.5\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 56.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.1\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 54 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.3\).
