You may find it helpful to convert the densities to frequencies by multiplying each density by both the total sample size (\(n=50\)) and the width of the bar (2).


The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.88) There are 44 measurements less than 48. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<48]=0.88\).
  2. (ANS=0.16) There are 8 measurements more than 46. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>46]=0.16\).
  3. (ANS=0.08) There are 4 measurements closer than 2 units from 48. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-48|<2]=0.08\).
  4. (ANS=0.9) There are 45 measurements further than 1 units from 45. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-45|>1]=0.9\).
  5. (ANS=50) A boundary at \(b=50\) has 46 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<50]=0.92\).
  6. (ANS=48) A boundary at \(b=48\) has 6 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>48]=0.12\).
  7. (ANS=3) An interval with radius \(r=3\) around center 47 contains 9 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-47|<3]=0.18\).
  8. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 48 excludes 46 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-48|>2]=0.92\).

ANSWERS: 0.88, 0.16, 0.08, 0.9, 50, 48, 3, 2
