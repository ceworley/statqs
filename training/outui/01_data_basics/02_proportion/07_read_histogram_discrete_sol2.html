The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

You may also find it is helpful to determine frequencies (counts). You do this by multiplying each relative frequency by the total number of measurements.



  1. (ANS=0.3) There are 30 measurements less than 3.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=\frac{30}{100}=0.3\).
  2. (ANS=0.39) There are 39 measurements more than 6.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>6.5]=\frac{39}{100}=0.39\).
  3. (ANS=0.1) There are 10 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 4. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-4|<0.5]=\frac{10}{100}=0.1\).
  4. (ANS=0.68) There are 68 measurements further than 1.5 units from 3. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=\frac{68}{100}=0.68\).
  5. (ANS=7.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.82\cdot100=82\). A boundary at \(b=7.5\) has 82 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=0.82\).
  6. (ANS=5.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.49\cdot100=49\). A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 49 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>5.5]=0.49\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 2.5 contains 22 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|<1]=0.22\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 7 excludes 79 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-7|>0.5]=0.79\).
