The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

You may also find it is helpful to determine frequencies (counts). You do this by multiplying each relative frequency by the total number of measurements.



  1. (ANS=0.86) There are 86 measurements less than 7.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=\frac{86}{100}=0.86\).
  2. (ANS=0.72) There are 72 measurements more than 2.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=\frac{72}{100}=0.72\).
  3. (ANS=0.12) There are 12 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 2. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-2|<0.5]=\frac{12}{100}=0.12\).
  4. (ANS=0.42) There are 42 measurements further than 2.5 units from 5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-5|>2.5]=\frac{42}{100}=0.42\).
  5. (ANS=3.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.44\cdot100=44\). A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 44 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=0.44\).
  6. (ANS=6.5) First, convert the proportion into a count. \(0.24\cdot100=24\). A boundary at \(b=6.5\) has 24 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>6.5]=0.24\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 contains 16 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=0.16\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 excludes 62 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=0.62\).
