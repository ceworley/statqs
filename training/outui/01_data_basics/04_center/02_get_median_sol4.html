To determine the median by hand, you first sort the sample. \[\text{sort}(\mathbf{x})=55.15, 61.16, 61.95, 62.25, 63.25, 65.57, 67.13, 67.96, 68.12, 68.12, 70.76, 71.49\] If the sample size is odd, just find the number in the middle number (the \(\frac{n+1}{2}\)th value). If the sample size is even, determine the mean of the middle two numbers (the \(\frac{n}{2}\)th and \(\left(\frac{n}{2}+1\right)\)th values).


In a spreadsheet, you can use the MEDIAN function. You can see a solution spreadsheet here.

In R, you can use the MEDIAN function.
