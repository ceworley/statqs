01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v2

A random procedure generated 75 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
60 — 62 22
62 — 64 21
64 — 66 11
66 — 68 7
68 — 70 14

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<66]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>64]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-65|<3]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-65|>1]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 43\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 32\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-65|<r] = 11\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-66|>r] = 57\).
