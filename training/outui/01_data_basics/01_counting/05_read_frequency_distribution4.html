A random procedure generated 120 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency

31 — 31.5 0
31.5 — 32 2
32 — 32.5 6
32.5 — 33 9
33 — 33.5 10
33.5 — 34 14

34.5 — 35 57

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<32.5]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>32]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32.5|<0.5]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-33.75|>0.25]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 3\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 78\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32.25|<r] = 17\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32|>r] = 103\).
