The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=59) There are 59 measurements less than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=59\).
  2. (ANS=58) There are 58 measurements more than 1.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=58\).
  3. (ANS=9) There are 9 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=9\).
  4. (ANS=55) There are 55 measurements further than 0.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>0.5]=55\).
  5. (ANS=3.5) A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 33 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=33\).
  6. (ANS=3.5) A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 37 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=37\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 contains 32 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<1.5]=32\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 3.5 excludes 50 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>1]=50\).
