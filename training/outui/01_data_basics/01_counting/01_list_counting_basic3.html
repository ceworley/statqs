01_data_basics/01_counting/01_list_counting_basic_v3

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 55, 57, 58, 52, 55, 50, 50, 55, 50, 51 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are less than 50? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<50]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 50? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le50]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are greater than 55? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>55]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 55? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge55]\).
  5. If 0 of the measurements are less than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 0\).
  6. If 8 of the measurements are less than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 8\).
  7. If 6 of the measurements are greater than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 6\).
  8. If 2 of the measurements are greater than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 2\).
