01_data_basics/01_counting/01_list_counting_basic_v5

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 84, 82, 86, 85, 82, 89, 82, 82, 87, 85 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are less than 82? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<82]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 86? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le86]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are greater than 82? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>82]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 82? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge82]\).
  5. If 0 of the measurements are less than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 0\).
  6. If 7 of the measurements are less than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 7\).
  7. If 5 of the measurements are greater than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 5\).
  8. If 2 of the measurements are greater than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 2\).
