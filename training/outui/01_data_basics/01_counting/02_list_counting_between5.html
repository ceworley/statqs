01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v5

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 56, 58, 53, 53, 55, 52, 55, 53, 59, 56 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 50.5 to 58.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>50.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<58.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[50.5<\mathbf{x}<58.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 54.5 to 55.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<54.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>55.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(54.5<\mathbf{x}<55.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 4 units from 55.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55.5\big|<4\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 3.5 units from 56? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56\big|>3.5\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 54 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|<r\right] ~=~ 5\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 56.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 5\).
