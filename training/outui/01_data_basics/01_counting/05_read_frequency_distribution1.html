01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v1

A random procedure generated 120 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
25 — 26 49
26 — 27 10
27 — 28 6
28 — 29 9
29 — 30 46

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<27]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>28]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-27.5|<1.5]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-27.5|>0.5]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 74\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 55\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-28.5|<r] = 9\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-26.5|>r] = 110\).
