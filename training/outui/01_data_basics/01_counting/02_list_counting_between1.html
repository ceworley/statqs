01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v1

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 29, 29, 29, 25, 27, 27, 23, 23, 26, 25 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 24.5 to 29.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>24.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<29.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[24.5<\mathbf{x}<29.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 25.5 to 28.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<25.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>28.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(25.5<\mathbf{x}<28.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 2.5 units from 27? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-27\big|<2.5\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 3.5 units from 26? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-26\big|>3.5\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 23 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-23\big|<r\right] ~=~ 4\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 27 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-27\big|>r\right] ~=~ 8\).
