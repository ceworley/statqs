The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=118) There are 118 measurements less than 82. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<82]=118\).
  2. (ANS=13) There are 13 measurements more than 78. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>78]=13\).
  3. (ANS=38) There are 38 measurements closer than 4 units from 78. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-78|<4]=38\).
  4. (ANS=109) There are 109 measurements further than 1 units from 73. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-73|>1]=109\).
  5. (ANS=78) A boundary at \(b=78\) has 107 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<78]=107\).
  6. (ANS=76) A boundary at \(b=76\) has 19 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>76]=19\).
  7. (ANS=4) An interval with radius \(r=4\) around center 78 contains 38 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-78|<4]=38\).
  8. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 78 excludes 107 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-78|>2]=107\).
