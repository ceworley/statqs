The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=59) There are 59 measurements less than 27. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<27]=59\).
  2. (ANS=55) There are 55 measurements more than 28. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>28]=55\).
  3. (ANS=25) There are 25 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 27.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-27.5|<1.5]=25\).
  4. (ANS=114) There are 114 measurements further than 0.5 units from 27.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-27.5|>0.5]=114\).
  5. (ANS=29) A boundary at \(b=29\) has 74 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<29]=74\).
  6. (ANS=28) A boundary at \(b=28\) has 55 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>28]=55\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 28.5 contains 9 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-28.5|<0.5]=9\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 26.5 excludes 110 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-26.5|>0.5]=110\).
