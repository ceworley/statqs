01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v3

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 37, 37, 32, 31, 31, 31, 32, 39, 31, 38 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 38.5 to 39.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>38.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<39.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[38.5<\mathbf{x}<39.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 33.5 to 35.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<33.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>35.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(33.5<\mathbf{x}<35.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 2 units from 35.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-35.5\big|<2\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 1 unit from 36.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-36.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 38 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-38\big|<r\right] ~=~ 1\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 32 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-32\big|>r\right] ~=~ 8\).
