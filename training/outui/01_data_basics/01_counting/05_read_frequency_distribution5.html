01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v5

A random procedure generated 120 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
50 — 52 50
52 — 54 6
54 — 56 11
56 — 58 2
58 — 60 51

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<54]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>56]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-55|<1]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-57|>1]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 56\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 53\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-53|<r] = 6\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-57|>r] = 118\).
