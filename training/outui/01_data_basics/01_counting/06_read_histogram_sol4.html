The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=25) There are 25 measurements less than 62. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<62]=25\).
  2. (ANS=35) There are 35 measurements more than 62. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>62]=35\).
  3. (ANS=17) There are 17 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 63.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63.5|<0.5]=17\).
  4. (ANS=50) There are 50 measurements further than 0.5 units from 61.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-61.5|>0.5]=50\).
  5. (ANS=62) A boundary at \(b=62\) has 25 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<62]=25\).
  6. (ANS=63) A boundary at \(b=63\) has 23 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>63]=23\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 63.5 contains 17 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63.5|<0.5]=17\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 63 excludes 31 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63|>1]=31\).
