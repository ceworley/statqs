First, determine the interval of typical measurements.

\[\begin{aligned} \text{interval of typical measurements} &= (\mu-2\sigma,\,\mu+2\sigma)\\ &= (68,72) \end{aligned}\]

Now, determine how many measurements (and divide by 200 for what proportion) are either less than 68 or more than 72. You’ll want to use a computer.

R

x = read.csv("check_interval_typical_measurements.csv")$x
n = length(x)
count_outside = sum(x<68 | x>72)
prop_outside = count_outside/n
print(prop_outside)
## [1] 0.07

In R, the “|” operator means “or”.

You could also write the equality as an absolute deviation from the mean. Any measurement more than 2 units from 70 (in either direction) would be outside the interval.

x = read.csv("check_interval_typical_measurements.csv")$x
n = length(x)
count_outside = sum( abs(x-70)>2 )
prop_outside = count_outside/n
print(prop_outside)
## [1] 0.07

Spreadsheet
