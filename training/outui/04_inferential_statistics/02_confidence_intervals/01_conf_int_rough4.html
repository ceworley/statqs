A scientist has weighed 58 specimens of a newly discovered organism. Those weights have a sample mean of \(\bar{x} =67.5\) grams and a sample standard deviation of \(s=20.2\) grams. The scientist hopes to construct a 95% confidence interval of the organismâ€™s population mean (\(\mu\)).

The scientist will later consult a statistician for a more precise method, but for now she will use a quick method to estimate the 95% confidence interval: \[\bar{x}\pm \frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}} \]

(You can round to nearest 0.1 grams.)


  1. Determine the lower boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\bar{x}-\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}\).
  2. Determine the upper boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\bar{x}+\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}\).
