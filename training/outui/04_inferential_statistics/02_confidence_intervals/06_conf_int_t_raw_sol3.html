x = c(452, 390, 413, 423, 413, 411, 407, 414, 445, 385, 406, 397, 422, 397, 420, 372)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.96   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=16\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=410.44\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=20.44\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{20.44}{\sqrt{16}}=5.11\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=16\), then \(\text{df}=15\), and \(P(|T|<2.25)=0.96\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=2.25\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(2.25)(20.44)}{\sqrt{16}}=11.49\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=410.44-11.49=398.95\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=410.44+11.49=421.93\).
