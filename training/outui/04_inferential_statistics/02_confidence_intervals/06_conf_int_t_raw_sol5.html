x = c(681, 697, 693, 612, 615, 580, 688, 753, 672, 684, 702, 664)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.95   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=12\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=670.08\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=47.04\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{47.04}{\sqrt{12}}=13.58\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=12\), then \(\text{df}=11\), and \(P(|T|<2.2)=0.95\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=2.2\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(2.2)(47.04)}{\sqrt{12}}=29.89\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=670.08-29.89=640.2\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=670.08+29.89=699.97\).
