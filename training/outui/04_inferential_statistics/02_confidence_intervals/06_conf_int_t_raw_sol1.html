x = c(582, 665, 639, 617, 678, 644, 681, 638, 636, 600, 689, 646, 646, 637, 666, 630, 582, 663, 621, 633, 678, 688, 707, 644, 704, 638, 645, 623, 668, 638)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.99   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=30\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=647.53\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=31.4\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{31.4}{\sqrt{30}}=5.73\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=30\), then \(\text{df}=29\), and \(P(|T|<2.76)=0.99\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=2.76\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(2.76)(31.4)}{\sqrt{30}}=15.8\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=647.53-15.8=631.73\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=647.53+15.8=663.34\).
