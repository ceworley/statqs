We can approximate a 95% confidence interval (where the 95% refers to the confidence level: how frequently these intervals straddle the population mean) by using \(\bar{x}\pm \frac{2 s}{\sqrt{n}}\). \[\text{lower boundary} = \bar{x}-\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}\] \[\text{upper boundary} = \bar{x}+\frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}\] where \(\bar{x}\) is the sample mean, \(s\) is the sample standard deviation, and \(n\) is the sample size. The 2 comes from the fact that 95% of normal measurements land within 2 standard deviations from the mean.

The quantity that we subtract from or add to the sample mean is called the margin of error. \[\text{margin of error} = \frac{2s}{\sqrt{n}}\]

When using a confidence level of 0.95, knowing \(s\) will be approximately 0.62, and wanting the margin of error to be approximately 0.0068, how large does the sample size need to be?

You can round your answer to two significant digits.
