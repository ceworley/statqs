This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 6 from a set of size 11.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 11 \\\\ r &= 6 \\\\ {_{11}C_{6}} &= \frac{11!}{(11-6)!\cdot 6! } \\\\ &= \frac{11!}{5! \cdot 6!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 11\cdot10\cdot9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6}{ 6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 462 \end{aligned}\]
