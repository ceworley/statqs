02_probability/01_overview/q5_binomial_v3

In some situation, each trial has 0.44 probability of success. There will be 9 trials. (Thus the number of successes will follow a binomial distribution.)


  1. What is the probability of getting exactly 5 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X = 5]\).
  2. What is the probability of getting exactly 2 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X = 2]\).
  3. What is the probability of getting more than 2 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X > 2]\).
  4. What is the probability of getting at least 2 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X \ge 2]\).
  5. What is the probability of getting less than 2 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X < 2]\).
  6. What is the probability of getting at most 2 successes? In other words, determine \(\mathbb{P}[X \le 2]\).
  7. Determine the mean number of successes.
  8. Determine the standard deviation of successes.
