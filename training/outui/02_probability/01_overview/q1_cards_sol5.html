The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower}]=\frac{113}{390}=0.29\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{flower given violet}]=\frac{12}{71}=0.169\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{violet}]=\frac{71}{390}=0.182\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{wheel or violet}]=\frac{147+71-17}{390}=0.515\)
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{black given flower}] = 0.204\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{black given wheel}] = 0.333\), so a wheel is more likely to be black than a flower is. / .
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{green given wheel}]=\frac{44}{147}=0.299\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt and black}]=\frac{13}{390}=0.0333\)

ANSWERS: 0.29, 0.169, 0.182, 0.515, c(FALSE, TRUE), 0.299, 0.0333
