To determine the median by hand, you first sort the sample. \[\text{sort}(\mathbf{x})=45.43, 47.08, 47.67, 48.08, 48.93, 49.28, 49.59, 49.8, 49.93, 49.95, 49.97, 49.99\] If the sample size is odd, just find the number in the middle number (the \(\frac{n+1}{2}\)th value). If the sample size is even, determine the mean of the middle two numbers (the \(\frac{n}{2}\)th and \(\left(\frac{n}{2}+1\right)\)th values).


In a spreadsheet, you can use the MEDIAN function. You can see a solution spreadsheet here.

In R, you can use the MEDIAN function.
