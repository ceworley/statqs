x = c(276, 369, 346, 316, 285, 278, 349, 283)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.9   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=8\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=312.75\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=37.43\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{37.43}{\sqrt{8}}=13.23\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=8\), then \(\text{df}=7\), and \(P(|T|<1.89)=0.9\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=1.89\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(1.89)(37.43)}{\sqrt{8}}=25.07\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=312.75-25.07=287.68\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=312.75+25.07=337.82\).
