x = c(282, 312, 279, 315, 301, 307, 340, 323, 277, 212, 246, 334, 356, 329)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.8   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=14\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=300.93\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=38.85\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{38.85}{\sqrt{14}}=10.38\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=14\), then \(\text{df}=13\), and \(P(|T|<1.35)=0.8\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=1.35\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(1.35)(38.85)}{\sqrt{14}}=14.02\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=300.93-14.02=286.91\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=300.93+14.02=314.95\).
