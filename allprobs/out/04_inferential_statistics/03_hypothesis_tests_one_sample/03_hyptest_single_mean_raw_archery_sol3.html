Determine the sample statistics (\(\bar{x}\) and \(s\)). I would recommend using a spreadsheet or R (or another computer-based method). \[\bar{x} = -18.69\] \[s = 37.67 \] Evaluate the \(t\) statistic.

\[t = \frac{|-18.69-0|}{(37.67)/\sqrt{36}} = 2.98 \] Restate the \(p\)-value. \[p\text{-value} = P\left(|T|>2.98\right) \] Remember, the degree of freedom is one less than the sample size. \[\text{df} = 35 \]

At this point, there are various ways to determine the \(p\)-value. The least accurate way is to use the \(t\)-table. Go to the row with \(\text{df}=n-1=36-1=35\).


Luckily, in this case, the table has a listed \(t\) closely matching 2.98 in the row with df=35. This tells us that \[P(|T|>2.98)\approx0.005\]

The more accurate \(p\)-value is 0.0052459. This can be calculated using a computer; for example, you can use this web app (for full accuracy, youâ€™ll need the more precise value of \(t=2.9775638\)). You can also use a spreadsheet or R.
