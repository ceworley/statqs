First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(C\) axis by adding integer multiples of 13 to 43. We know to shade the the outsides because values far from 43 satisfy the condition \(\big|C-43\big|>d\) (regardless of the exact value of \(d\)). We don’t know exactly where to place the boundaries, but we know the two-tail area is 0.24.


As an intermediate step, let’s find \(z\) such that \(P(|Z|>z)=0.24\). First, we need to evaluate \(P(Z<z)\).

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= \frac{2-P(|Z|>z)}{2} \\ \\ &= \frac{2-0.24}{2} \\ \\ &= 0.88 \end{aligned}\]

You could have also drawn some pictures… we know there is symmetry and all the areas should add to 1.



We find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.88\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

Either 1.17 or 1.18 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=1.175\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(d\) score. \[\begin{aligned} d &= z\sigma \\ &= (1.175)(13) \\ &= 15.27 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.
