First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(C\) axis by adding integer multiples of 5 to 91. We know to shade the between \(89\) and \(91\) because those values satisfy the condition \(89 < X < 91\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{89-91}{5} \\\\ &= -0.4 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{91-91}{5} \\\\ &= 0 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(89 < X < 91\right) ~=~ P(-0.4<Z<0)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-0.4<Z<0)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-0.4<Z<0) &= P(Z<0)-P(Z<-0.4)\\ &= 0.5-0.3446 \\ &= 0.1554 \end{aligned}\]
