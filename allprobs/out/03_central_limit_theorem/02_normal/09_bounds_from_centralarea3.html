Random variable \(H\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 4.1\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 1.3\). \[H \sim \text{N}(4.1,\,1.3) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|H-4.1\big|<d\right) = 0.82\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands within those boundaries 82% of the time.
  2. Let \(h_1 = 4.1-d\) and \(h_2 = 4.1+d\), such that \(P\left(h_1 < H < h_2\right) = 0.82\). Evaluate \(h_1\).
  3. Using the definitions above, evaluate \(h_2\)
