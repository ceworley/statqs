Determine the sample statistics (\(\bar{x}\) and \(s\)). I would recommend using a spreadsheet or R (or another computer-based method). \[\bar{x} = -75.42\] \[s = 113.2 \] Evaluate the \(t\) statistic.

\[t = \frac{|-75.42-0|}{(113.2)/\sqrt{24}} = 3.26 \] Restate the \(p\)-value. \[p\text{-value} = P\left(|T|>3.26\right) \] Remember, the degree of freedom is one less than the sample size. \[\text{df} = 23 \]

At this point, there are various ways to determine the \(p\)-value. The least accurate way is to use the \(t\)-table. Go to the row with \(\text{df}=n-1=24-1=23\).


If we go to the row with df=23, we can see that our calculated \(t\), 3.26, is between 3.2 and 3.48. Thus, we know \(P(|T|>3.26)\) is between 0.002 and 0.004. You could get pretty close by using linear interpolation. \[P(|T|>3.26)\approx \left(\frac{3.26-3.2}{3.48-3.2}\right)\left(0.002-0.004\right)+0.004 = 0.00357\]
