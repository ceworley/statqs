We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (59)(29.67) ~=~ 1750.53 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.56)\sqrt{59} = 11.9825874\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 1750.53 and a standard deviation of 11.9825874. \[\sum{X} \sim N(1750.53,\,11.9825874) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{9.47}{11.9825874} = 0.7903135 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.79\) or \(z=0.8\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.7903135) = 0.4293447 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.79) = 0.4296 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.8) = 0.4238 \]
