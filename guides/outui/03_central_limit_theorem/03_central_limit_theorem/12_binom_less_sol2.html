We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (54)(0.67) ~=~ 36.18\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(54)(0.67)(0.33)} ~=~ 3.4553437 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{33.5-36.18}{3.4553437} \\\\ &= -0.7756103 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.7756103) ~=~ 0.2189896 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.78) ~=~ 0.2177 \] \[P(Z < -0.77) ~=~ 0.2206 \]
