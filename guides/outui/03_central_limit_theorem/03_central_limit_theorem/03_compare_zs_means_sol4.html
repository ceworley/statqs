The formula to determine the \(z\)-score of a sample mean is the ratio with numerator the difference between measurement and population mean and denominator the standard error of the mean.

\[z = \frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \]

The highest \(z\)-score (furthest right on number line) corresponds to the most unusually large sample mean.

The smallest \(z\)-score (furthest left on number line) corresponds to the most unusually small sample mean.

The smallest absolute \(z\)-score corresponds to the most usually sized sample mean.

The largest absolute \(z\)-score corresponds to the most unusually sized sample mean.

To do this in R:

data = read.csv("fruit.csv", as.is=TRUE)
fruit = data$fruit
mu = data$mu
sigma = data$sigma
n = data$n
xbar = data$xbar

SEM = sigma/sqrt(n)
z = round( (xbar-mu)/SEM ,2)
cum_prob = round( pnorm(z) ,4)
data.frame(data,SEM,z,cum_prob,abs(z))
