We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.72\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.72)(0.28)}{n}} ~=~ 0.070993 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5625-0.72}{0.070993} \\\\ &= -2.2185299 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -2.2185299) ~=~ 0.9867406 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -2.22) ~=~ 0.9868 \] \[P(Z > -2.21) ~=~ 0.9864 \]
