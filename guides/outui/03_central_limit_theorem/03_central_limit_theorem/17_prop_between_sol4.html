We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.66\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.66)(0.34)}{166}} ~=~ 0.0367669 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.6235-0.66}{0.0367669} \\\\ &= -0.9929038 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.6536-0.66}{0.0367669} \\\\ &= -0.1736762 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.9929038 < Z < -0.1736762) ~=~ 0.2706815 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-1 < Z < -0.18) ~=~ 0.2699 \] \[P(-0.99 < Z < -0.17) ~=~ 0.2714 \] \[P(-1 < Z < -0.17) ~=~ 0.2675 \] \[P(-0.99 < Z < -0.18) ~=~ 0.2738 \]
