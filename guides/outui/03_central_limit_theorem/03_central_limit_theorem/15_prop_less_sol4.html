We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.4\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.4)(0.6)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0454859 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.4267-0.4}{0.0454859} \\\\ &= 0.5869953 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < 0.5869953) ~=~ 0.7213966 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < 0.58) ~=~ 0.719 \] \[P(Z < 0.59) ~=~ 0.7224 \]
