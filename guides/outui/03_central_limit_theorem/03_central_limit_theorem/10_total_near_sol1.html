We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (94)(64.97) ~=~ 6107.18 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (8.86)\sqrt{94} = 85.9008871\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 6107.18 and a standard deviation of 85.9008871. \[\sum{X} \sim N(6107.18,\,85.9008871) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{92.82}{85.9008871} = 1.0805476 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.08\) or \(z=1.09\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 1.0805476) = 0.7201016 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.08) = 0.7198 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.09) = 0.7242 \]
