We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 43.02 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{7.79}{\sqrt{182}} = 0.5774332\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 43.02 and a standard deviation of 0.5774332. \[\bar{X} \sim N(43.02,\,0.5774332) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.95}{0.5774332} = 1.6452119 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.64\) or \(z=1.65\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 1.6452119) = 0.0999261 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.64) = 0.101 \] \[P(|Z| > 1.65) = 0.099 \]
