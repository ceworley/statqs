We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (192)(0.31) ~=~ 59.52\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(192)(0.31)(0.69)} ~=~ 6.4084944 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{53.5-59.52}{6.4084944} \\\\ &= -0.9393782 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -0.9393782) ~=~ 0.8262317 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -0.94) ~=~ 0.8264 \] \[P(Z > -0.93) ~=~ 0.8238 \]
