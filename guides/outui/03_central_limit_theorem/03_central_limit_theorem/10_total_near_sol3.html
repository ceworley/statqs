We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (149)(58.67) ~=~ 8741.83 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.16)\sqrt{149} = 14.1596045\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 8741.83 and a standard deviation of 14.1596045. \[\sum{X} \sim N(8741.83,\,14.1596045) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{18.17}{14.1596045} = 1.2832279 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.28\) or \(z=1.29\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| < 1.2832279) = 0.8005878 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.28) = 0.7994 \] \[P(|Z| < 1.29) = 0.803 \]
