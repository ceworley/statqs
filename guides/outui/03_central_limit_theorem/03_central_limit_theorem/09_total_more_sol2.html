We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (156)(73.71) ~=~ 11498.76 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (7.03)\sqrt{156} = 87.8046719\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 11498.76 and a standard deviation of 87.8046719. \[\sum{X} \sim N(11498.76,\,87.8046719) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{11400-11498.76}{87.8046719} = -1.1247693 \]

You can round that to \(z=-1.13\) or \(z=-1.12\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -1.1247693) = 0.8696566 \] \[P(Z > -1.13) = 0.8708 \] \[P(Z > -1.12) = 0.8686 \]
