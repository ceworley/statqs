We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (94)(0.83) ~=~ 78.02\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(94)(0.83)(0.17)} ~=~ 3.6418951 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{75.5-78.02}{3.6418951} \\\\ &= -0.6919474 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -0.6919474) ~=~ 0.7555148 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -0.7) ~=~ 0.758 \] \[P(Z > -0.69) ~=~ 0.7549 \]
