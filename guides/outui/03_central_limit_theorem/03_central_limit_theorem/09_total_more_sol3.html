We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (144)(78.58) ~=~ 11315.52 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (1.59)\sqrt{144} = 19.08\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 11315.52 and a standard deviation of 19.08. \[\sum{X} \sim N(11315.52,\,19.08) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{11300-11315.52}{19.08} = -0.8134172 \]

You can round that to \(z=-0.82\) or \(z=-0.81\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -0.8134172) = 0.7920105 \] \[P(Z > -0.82) = 0.7939 \] \[P(Z > -0.81) = 0.791 \]
