We use the central limit formulas for a random total. \[\mu_{\sum{X}} ~=~ n\cdot\mu_{X} ~=~ (71)(51.33) ~=~ 3644.43 \] \[\sigma_{\sum{X}} = \sigma_{X}\sqrt{n} = (12.34)\sqrt{71} = 103.9786882\] So, we think the random total is normally distributed with a mean of 3644.43 and a standard deviation of 103.9786882. \[\sum{X} \sim N(3644.43,\,103.9786882) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{25.57}{103.9786882} = 0.2459158 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.24\) or \(z=0.25\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.2459158) = 0.8057474 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.24) = 0.8104 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.25) = 0.8026 \]
