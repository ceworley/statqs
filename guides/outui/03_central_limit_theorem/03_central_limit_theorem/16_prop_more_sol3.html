We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.64\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.64)(0.36)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0368143 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.6853-0.64}{0.0368143} \\\\ &= 1.2304995 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 1.2304995) ~=~ 0.1092551 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 1.23) ~=~ 0.1093 \] \[P(Z > 1.24) ~=~ 0.1075 \]
