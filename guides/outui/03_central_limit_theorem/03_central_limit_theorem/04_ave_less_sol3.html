We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 58.97 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{14}{\sqrt{762}} = 0.5071665\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 58.97 and a standard deviation of 0.5071665. \[\bar{X} \sim N(58.97,\,0.5071665) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{59.52-58.97}{0.5071665} = 1.0844565 \]

You can round that to \(z=1.08\) or \(z=1.09\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z < 1.0844565) = 0.8609188 \] \[P(Z < 1.08) = 0.8599 \] \[P(Z < 1.09) = 0.8621 \]
