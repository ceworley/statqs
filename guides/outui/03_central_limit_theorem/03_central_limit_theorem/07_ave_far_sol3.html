We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 65.24 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{2.97}{\sqrt{31}} = 0.5334277\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 65.24 and a standard deviation of 0.5334277. \[\bar{X} \sim N(65.24,\,0.5334277) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{0.43}{0.5334277} = 0.8061073 \]

You can round that to \(z=0.8\) or \(z=0.81\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(|Z| > 0.8061073) = 0.420181 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.8) = 0.4238 \] \[P(|Z| > 0.81) = 0.418 \]
