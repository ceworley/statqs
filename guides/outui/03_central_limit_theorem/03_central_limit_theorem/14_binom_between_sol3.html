We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (46)(0.36) ~=~ 16.56\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(46)(0.36)(0.64)} ~=~ 3.2555184 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{x_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{16.5-16.56}{3.2555184} \\\\ &= -0.0184302 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{x_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{18.5-16.56}{3.2555184} \\\\ &= 0.5959112 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(-0.0184302 < Z < 0.5959112) ~=~ 0.2317349 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(-0.02 < Z < 0.59) ~=~ 0.2304 \] \[P(-0.01 < Z < 0.6) ~=~ 0.2297 \] \[P(-0.02 < Z < 0.6) ~=~ 0.2264 \] \[P(-0.01 < Z < 0.59) ~=~ 0.2337 \]
