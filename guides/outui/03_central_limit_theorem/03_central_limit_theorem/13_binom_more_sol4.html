We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (168)(0.25) ~=~ 42\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(168)(0.25)(0.75)} ~=~ 5.6124861 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{37.5-42}{5.6124861} \\\\ &= -0.8017837 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > -0.8017837) ~=~ 0.788661 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > -0.81) ~=~ 0.791 \] \[P(Z > -0.8) ~=~ 0.7881 \]
