We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (169)(3.5) \\ &= 591.5 \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (1.7078251)\sqrt{169} \\ &= 22.2017267 \end{aligned}\]

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

\[\sum X \sim N(591.5,\,22.2017267) \]

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 169 rolls of 6-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.


Find the appropriate \(z\) score.

\[z ~=~ \frac{(\sum x)-\mu_{ \sum X}}{\sigma_{ \sum X}} ~=~ \frac{569.5-591.5}{22.2017267} ~=~ -0.9909139\]

You can round this to either -1 or -0.99. I will continue with the unrounded \(z\) score. We now rephrase the question as a standard normal probability.

\[P\left(\sum X < 569.5\right) ~=~ P(Z<-0.9909139) \] \[P(Z<-0.9909139) ~=~ \, ?\]

You can use the \(z\) table to find the probability.

\[P(Z<-0.9909139) ~=~ 0.1608638\]

\[P(Z<-1) ~=~ 0.1587\] \[P(Z<-0.99) ~=~ 0.1611\]
