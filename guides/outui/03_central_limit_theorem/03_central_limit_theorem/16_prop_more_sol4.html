We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.5\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.5)(0.5)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0800641 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5513-0.5}{0.0800641} \\\\ &= 0.6407368 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z > 0.6407368) ~=~ 0.2608469 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z > 0.64) ~=~ 0.2611 \] \[P(Z > 0.65) ~=~ 0.2578 \]
