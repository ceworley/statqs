We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.32\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.32)(0.68)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0358828 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (The common notation is \(\hat{p}\) for a specific proportion.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2988-0.32}{0.0358828} \\\\ &= -0.5908126 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < -0.5908126) ~=~ 0.277323 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < -0.6) ~=~ 0.2743 \] \[P(Z < -0.59) ~=~ 0.2776 \]
