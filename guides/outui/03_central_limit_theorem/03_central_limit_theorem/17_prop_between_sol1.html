We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. \[\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.48\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.48)(0.52)}{37}} ~=~ 0.0821337 \]

We determine both \(z\)-scores. We get the first \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5-0.48}{0.0821337} \\\\ &= 0.2435054 \end{aligned}\]

We get the second \(z\)-score.

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5541-0.48}{0.0821337} \\\\ &= 0.901628 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(0.2435054 < Z < 0.901628) ~=~ 0.2201797 \]

Of course, you could have rounded the \(z\)-scores, so any of the following will also get credit. \[P(0.24 < Z < 0.9) ~=~ 0.2211 \] \[P(0.25 < Z < 0.91) ~=~ 0.2199 \] \[P(0.24 < Z < 0.91) ~=~ 0.2172 \] \[P(0.25 < Z < 0.9) ~=~ 0.2238 \]
