We determine the mean and standard deviation of the binomial distribution. \[\mu ~=~ np ~=~ (147)(0.69) ~=~ 101.43\] \[\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{np(1-p)} ~=~ \sqrt{(147)(0.69)(0.31)} ~=~ 5.6074326 \]

We determine a \(z\)-score. (In the de Moivre-Laplace notes, I used \(\sum x\) to emphasize that a binomial variable is a sum of Bernoulli trials. Here, I will just use \(x\) as the boundary for number of successes, because this is the more common notation.) \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{106.5-101.43}{5.6074326} \\\\ &= 0.9041571 \end{aligned}\]

Then, find the standard normal probability.

\[P(Z < 0.9041571) ~=~ 0.8170439 \]

Of course, you could have rounded \(z\), so the following will also get credit. \[P(Z < 0.9) ~=~ 0.8159 \] \[P(Z < 0.91) ~=~ 0.8186 \]
