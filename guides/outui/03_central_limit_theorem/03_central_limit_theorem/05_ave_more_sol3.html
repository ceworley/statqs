We use the central limit formulas for a random mean. \[\mu_{\bar{X}} = \mu_{X} = 77.07 \] \[\sigma_{\bar{X}} = \frac{\sigma_{X}}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{6.75}{\sqrt{521}} = 0.2957229\] So, we think the random mean is normally distributed with a mean of 77.07 and a standard deviation of 0.2957229. \[\bar{X} \sim N(77.07,\,0.2957229) \]

Calculate a \(z\)-score for the boundary. \[z = \frac{76.79-77.07}{0.2957229} = -0.9468324 \]

You can round that to \(z=-0.95\) or \(z=-0.94\). Any of the following probabilities will get credit. \[P(Z > -0.9468324) = 0.8281379 \] \[P(Z > -0.95) = 0.8289 \] \[P(Z > -0.94) = 0.8264 \]
