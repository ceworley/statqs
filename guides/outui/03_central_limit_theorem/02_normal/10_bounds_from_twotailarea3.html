03_central_limit_theorem/02_normal/10_bounds_from_twotailarea_v3

Random variable \(K\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 7.4\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 1.8\). \[K \sim \text{N}(7.4,\,1.8) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|K-7.4\big|>d\right) = 0.44\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands beyond those boundaries 44% of the time.
  2. Let \(k_1 = 7.4-d\), such that \(P\left(K < k_1\right) = \frac{0.44}{2}\). Evaluate \(k_1\).
  3. Let \(k_2 = 7.4+d\), such that \(P\left(K > k_2\right) = \frac{0.44}{2}\). Evaluate \(k_2\).
