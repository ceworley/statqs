First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(M\) axis by adding integer multiples of 12 to 44. We know to shade the between \(26\) and \(32\) because those values satisfy the condition \(26 < M < 32\).


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{m_1-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{26-44}{12} \\\\ &= -1.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{m_2-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{32-44}{12} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}\]

We rephrase our problem into a standard normal probability problem:

\[P\left(26 < M < 32\right) ~=~ P(-1.5<Z<-1)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(-1.5<Z<-1)\).

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(z_1 < Z < z_2) &= P(Z<z_2) - P(Z<z1) \\ P(-1.5<Z<-1) &= P(Z<-1)-P(Z<-1.5)\\ &= 0.1587-0.0668 \\ &= 0.0919 \end{aligned}\]
