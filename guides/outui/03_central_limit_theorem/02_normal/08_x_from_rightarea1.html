Random variable \(V\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 0.1\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.02\). \[V \sim \text{N}(0.1,\,0.02) \] Evaluate \(v\) such that \(P(V>v) = 0.03\). In other words, determine an lower boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 0.1 and standard deviation 0.02 lands over that boundary 3% of the time.
