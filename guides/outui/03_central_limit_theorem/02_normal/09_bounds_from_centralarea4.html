Random variable \(Q\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 140\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 40\). \[Q \sim \text{N}(140,\,40) \]


  1. Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P\left(\big|Q-140\big|<d\right) = 0.5\). In other words, determine the distance from mean of two boundaries such that this normal spinner lands within those boundaries 50% of the time.
  2. Let \(q_1 = 140-d\) and \(q_2 = 140+d\), such that \(P\left(q_1 < Q < q_2\right) = 0.5\). Evaluate \(q_1\).
  3. Using the definitions above, evaluate \(q_2\)
