So, we know the \(z\)-score is between -1 and 0. Remember, \(Z\) and \(z\) always refer to the standard normal variable.


By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\) more precisely.

Either -0.96 or -0.95 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-0.9542\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(c\) score. \[\begin{aligned} c &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 420+(-0.9542)(100) \\ &= 324.6 \end{aligned}\]
