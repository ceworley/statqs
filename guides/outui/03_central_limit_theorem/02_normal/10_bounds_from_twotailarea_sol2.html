First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(X\) axis by adding integer multiples of 120 to 530. We know to shade the the outsides because values far from 530 satisfy the condition \(\big|X-530\big|>d\) (regardless of the exact value of \(d\)). We don’t know exactly where to place the boundaries, but we know the two-tail area is 0.58.


As an intermediate step, let’s find \(z\) such that \(P(|Z|>z)=0.58\). First, we need to evaluate \(P(Z<z)\).

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= \frac{2-P(|Z|>z)}{2} \\ \\ &= \frac{2-0.58}{2} \\ \\ &= 0.71 \end{aligned}\]

You could have also drawn some pictures… we know there is symmetry and all the areas should add to 1.



We find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.71\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)

Either 0.55 or 0.56 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=0.5534\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(d\) score. \[\begin{aligned} d &= z\sigma \\ &= (0.5534)(120) \\ &= 66.41 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.



  1. We determined \(d=66.41\)
  2. It is easy to find \(x_1 = 530-66.41 = 463.59\)
  3. It is easy to find \(x_2 = 530+66.41 = 596.41\)
