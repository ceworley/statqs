Random variable \(D\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 2\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 0.6\). \[D \sim \text{N}(2,\,0.6) \] Evaluate \(d\) such that \(P(D<d) = 0.28\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 2 and standard deviation 0.6 lands under that boundary 28% of the time.
