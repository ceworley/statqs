First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(V\) axis by adding integer multiples of 120 to 950. We know to shade toward the right because large values of \(V\) satisfy the condition \(V>1160\).


We are given a specific \(v\) value as a boundary. (Remember, for random variables we use uppercase letters, but for specific values we use lowercase.) \[v = 1160 \] We calculate the \(z\) value of the boundary. \[\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{v-\mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{1160-950}{120} \\\\ &= 1.75 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because \[P(V>1160) ~=~ P(Z>1.75) \]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(Z>1.75)\).

To do this, you need to remember that right-area events are complementary to left-area events. \[P(Z>1.75) ~=~ 1-P(Z<1.75) \]

You can use a \(z\)-table.

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)

1.76 0.9608
1.77 0.9616

Thus, we find our answer. \[\begin{aligned} P(V>1160) &= 1-0.9599 \\ &= 0.0401 \end{aligned}\]
