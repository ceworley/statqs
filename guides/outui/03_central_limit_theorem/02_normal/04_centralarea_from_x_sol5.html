First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(G\) axis by adding integer multiples of 120 to 930. We know to shade the center because values near 930 satisfy the condition \(\big|G-930\big| < 60\). We draw the boundaries at \(930-60=870\) and \(930+60=990\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|G-930\big| = 60\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|G-930\big| < 60\right) ~=~ P(870<G<990)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{870-930}{120} \\\\ &= \frac{-60}{120} \\\\ &= -0.5 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{990-930}{120} \\\\ &= \frac{60}{120} \\\\ &= 0.5 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|G-930\big| < 60\right) ~=~ P(870<G<990) ~=~ P(-0.5<Z<0.5) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|<0.5\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|<0.5)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{60}{120} = 0.5\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|<d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| < \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|<0.5) &= 2 \times P(Z<0.5)-1 \\ &= 2 \times 0.6915-1 \\ &= 0.383 \end{aligned}\]
