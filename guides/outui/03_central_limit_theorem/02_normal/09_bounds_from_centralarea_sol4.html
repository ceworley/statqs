First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(Q\) axis by adding integer multiples of 40 to 140. We know to shade the the middle because values near 140 satisfy the condition \(\big|Q-140\big|<d\) (regardless of the exact value of \(d\)). We don’t know exactly where to place the boundaries, but we know the central area is 0.5.


As an intermediate step, let’s find \(z\) such that \(P(|Z|<z)=0.5\). First, we need to evaluate \(P(Z<z)\).

\[\begin{aligned} P(Z<z) &= \frac{1+P(|Z|<z)}{2} \\ \\ &= \frac{1+0.5}{2}\\ \\ &= 0.75 \end{aligned}\]

You could have also drawn some pictures… we know there is symmetry and all the areas should add to 1.



We find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.75\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

Either 0.67 or 0.68 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=0.6745\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(d\) score. \[\begin{aligned} d &= z\sigma \\ &= (0.6745)(40) \\ &= 26.98 \end{aligned}\]
