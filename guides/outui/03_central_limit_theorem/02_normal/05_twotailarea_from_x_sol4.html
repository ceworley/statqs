First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(V\) axis by adding integer multiples of 100 to 400. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 400 satisfy the condition \(\big|V-400\big| > 160\). We draw the boundaries at \(400-160=240\) and \(400+160=560\) because those are the solutions to \(\big|V-400\big| = 160\). We can also rephrase the probability.

\[P\left(\big|V-400\big| > 160\right) ~=~ P\big(V<240 \text{ OR } V>560\big)\]


We calculate the \(z\) values of the boundaries. Left boundary: \[\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{240-400}{100} \\\\ &= \frac{-160}{100} \\\\ &= -1.6 \end{aligned}\]

Right boundary:

\[\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{560-400}{100} \\\\ &= \frac{160}{100} \\\\ &= 1.6 \end{aligned}\]

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

\[P\left(\big|V-400\big| > 160\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>1.6\right)\]

So, we just need to evaluate \(P(|Z|>1.6)\). I will also point out that \(\frac{160}{100} = 1.6\). In general, if \(X\) is normally distributed, then: \[P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)\]

From here we have a formula that lets us use the \(z\) table. (We practiced this part before.) \[\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>1.6) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<1.6) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.9452 \\ &= 0.1096 \end{aligned}\]
