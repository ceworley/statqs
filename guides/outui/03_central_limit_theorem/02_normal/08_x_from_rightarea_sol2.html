First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(G\) axis by adding integer multiples of 0.22 to 0.98. We know to shade the right because high values of \(G\) satisfy the condition \(G>g\) (regardless of the exact value of \(g\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.73.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(G<g) &= 1-P(G>g) \\ &= 1-0.73 \\ &= 0.27 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.27\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)

Either -0.62 or -0.61 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-0.6128\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(g\) score. \[\begin{aligned} g &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.98+(-0.6128)(0.22) \\ &= 0.8452 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.


The tolerance for an acceptable answer was \(\pm 0.01\) from 0.8451811. So, anything between 0.8351811 and 0.8551811 was accepted.
