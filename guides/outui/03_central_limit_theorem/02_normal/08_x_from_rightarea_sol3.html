First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(B\) axis by adding integer multiples of 16 to 49. We know to shade the right because high values of \(B\) satisfy the condition \(B>b\) (regardless of the exact value of \(b\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.9.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(B<b) &= 1-P(B>b) \\ &= 1-0.9 \\ &= 0.1 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.1\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)
-1.31 0.0951

Either -1.29 or -1.28 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-1.2816\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(b\) score. \[\begin{aligned} b &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 49+(-1.2816)(16) \\ &= 28.5 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.


The tolerance for an acceptable answer was \(\pm 1\) from 28.495175. So, anything between 27.495175 and 29.495175 was accepted.
