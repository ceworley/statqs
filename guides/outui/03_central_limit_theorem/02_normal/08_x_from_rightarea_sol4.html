First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(F\) axis by adding integer multiples of 0.15 to 0.58. We know to shade the right because high values of \(F\) satisfy the condition \(F>f\) (regardless of the exact value of \(f\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.81.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(F<f) &= 1-P(F>f) \\ &= 1-0.81 \\ &= 0.19 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.19\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

\(z\) \(P(Z<z)\)

Either -0.88 or -0.87 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=-0.8779\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(f\) score. \[\begin{aligned} f &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.58+(-0.8779)(0.15) \\ &= 0.4483 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.


The tolerance for an acceptable answer was \(\pm 0.01\) from 0.4483156. So, anything between 0.4383156 and 0.4583156 was accepted.
