First, draw a sketch. We can label the \(V\) axis by adding integer multiples of 0.02 to 0.1. We know to shade the right because high values of \(V\) satisfy the condition \(V>v\) (regardless of the exact value of \(v\)). We donâ€™t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.03.


We know how to find the left area. \[\begin{aligned} P(V<v) &= 1-P(V>v) \\ &= 1-0.03 \\ &= 0.97 \end{aligned}\]


As an intermediate step, we find \(z\) such that \(P(Z<z)=0.97\).

By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\).

Either 1.88 or 1.89 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=1.8808\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(v\) score. \[\begin{aligned} v &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 0.1+(1.8808)(0.02) \\ &= 0.1376 \end{aligned}\]

We can also visualize this with a spinner.


The tolerance for an acceptable answer was \(\pm 0.01\) from 0.1376159. So, anything between 0.1276159 and 0.1476159 was accepted.
