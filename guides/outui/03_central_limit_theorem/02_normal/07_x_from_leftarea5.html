Random variable \(C\) is normally distributed with mean \(\mu = 420\) and standard deviation \(\sigma = 100\). \[C \sim \text{N}(420,\,100) \] Evaluate \(c\) such that \(P(C<c) = 0.17\). In other words, determine an upper boundary such that a normal spinner with mean 420 and standard deviation 100 lands on a value less than that boundary 17% of the time.
