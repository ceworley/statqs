So, we know the \(z\)-score is between 1 and 2. Remember, \(Z\) and \(z\) always refer to the standard normal variable.


By using the \(z\) table we can determine \(z\) more precisely.

Either 1.64 or 1.65 is a good estimation of \(z\), and either value will lead you to an acceptable answer. Using other tools, a more accurate value can be found. I will show the work with a more accurate value. \[z=1.6449\]

We now convert the \(z\) score into a \(v\) score. \[\begin{aligned} v &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 7.4+(1.6449)(1.6) \\ &= 10.03 \end{aligned}\]
