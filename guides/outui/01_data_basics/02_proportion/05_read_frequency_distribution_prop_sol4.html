The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.

  2. (ANS=0.5615 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 73 measurements more than 25. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>25]=\frac{73}{130}=0.5615\).
  3. (ANS=0.07692 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 10 measurements closer than 2.5 units from 37.5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37.5|<2.5]=0.07692\).
  4. (ANS=0.9231 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 120 measurements further than 2.5 units from 37.5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37.5|>2.5]=0.9231\).
  5. (ANS=40) First, \(0.6462 \cdot 130 = 84\). A boundary at \(b=40\) has 84 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<40]=0.6462\).
  6. (ANS=35) First, \(0.4308 \cdot 130 = 56\). A boundary at \(b=35\) has 56 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>35]=0.4308\).
  7. (ANS=2.5) First, \(0.07692 \cdot 130 = 10\). An interval with radius \(r=2.5\) around center 37.5 contains 10 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-37.5|<2.5]=0.07692\).
