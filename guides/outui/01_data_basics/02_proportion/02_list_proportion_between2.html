01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v2

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 29, 28, 25, 24, 24, 29, 27, 26, 26, 21 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 21.5 cm to 25.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>21.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<25.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[21.5<\mathbf{x}<25.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 25.5 cm to 27.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<25.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>27.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(25.5<\mathbf{x}<27.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 3.5 cm from 25 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-25\big|<3.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1 cm from 23.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-23.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 24.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-24.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.8\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 23.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-23.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.7\).
