The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.7933 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 119 measurements less than 42. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<42]=\frac{119}{150}=0.7933\).
  2. (ANS=0.02667 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 4 measurements more than 43.5. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>43.5]=\frac{4}{150}=0.02667\).
  3. (ANS=0.006667 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 1 measurements closer than 0.25 units from 43.75. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-43.75|<0.25]=0.006667\).
  4. (ANS=0.5333 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 80 measurements further than 1.25 units from 41.75. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-41.75|>1.25]=0.5333\).
  5. (ANS=44) First, \(0.98 \cdot 150 = 147\). A boundary at \(b=44\) has 147 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<44]=0.98\).
  6. (ANS=42) First, \(0.2067 \cdot 150 = 31\). A boundary at \(b=42\) has 31 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>42]=0.2067\).
  7. (ANS=1) First, \(0.1267 \cdot 150 = 19\). An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 43.5 contains 19 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-43.5|<1]=0.1267\).
  8. (ANS=0.75) First, \(0.76 \cdot 150 = 114\). An interval with radius \(r=0.75\) around center 41.75 excludes 114 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-41.75|<0.75]=0.76\).
