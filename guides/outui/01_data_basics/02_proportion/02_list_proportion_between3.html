01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v3

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 56, 59, 55, 57, 54, 59, 57, 58, 59, 54 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 55.5 cm to 56.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>55.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<56.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[55.5<\mathbf{x}<56.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 52.5 cm to 55.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<52.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>55.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(52.5<\mathbf{x}<55.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 1 cm from 57.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-57.5\big|<1\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 1 cm from 51.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-51.5\big|>1\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 55 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.4\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 55.5 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.4\).
