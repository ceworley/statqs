The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.82 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 82 measurements less than 80. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<80]=\frac{82}{100}=0.82\).
  2. (ANS=0.18 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 18 measurements more than 80. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>80]=\frac{18}{100}=0.18\).
  3. (ANS=0.08 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 8 measurements closer than 2.5 units from 82.5. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|<2.5]=0.08\).
  4. (ANS=0.77 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 77 measurements further than 5 units from 80. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-80|>5]=0.77\).
  5. (ANS=90) First, \(0.97 \cdot 100 = 97\). A boundary at \(b=90\) has 97 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<90]=0.97\).
  6. (ANS=75) First, \(0.33 \cdot 100 = 33\). A boundary at \(b=75\) has 33 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>75]=0.33\).
  7. (ANS=7.5) First, \(0.3 \cdot 100 = 30\). An interval with radius \(r=7.5\) around center 82.5 contains 30 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|<7.5]=0.3\).
  8. (ANS=5) First, \(0.85 \cdot 100 = 85\). An interval with radius \(r=5\) around center 85 excludes 85 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-85|<5]=0.85\).
