The first 4 questions involve adding up relative frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=0.6 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 42 measurements less than 48. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<48]=\frac{42}{70}=0.6\).
  2. (ANS=0.5857 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 41 measurements more than 44. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>44]=\frac{41}{70}=0.5857\).
  3. (ANS=0.04286 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 3 measurements closer than 1 units from 43. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-43|<1]=0.04286\).
  4. (ANS=0.9 \(\pm\) 0.01) There are 63 measurements further than 1 units from 47. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-47|>1]=0.9\).
  5. (ANS=46) First, \(0.5 \cdot 70 = 35\). A boundary at \(b=46\) has 35 measurements less than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<46]=0.5\).
  6. (ANS=44) First, \(0.5857 \cdot 70 = 41\). A boundary at \(b=44\) has 41 measurements more than it. So, \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>44]=0.5857\).
  7. (ANS=2) First, \(0.1286 \cdot 70 = 9\). An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 44 contains 9 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-44|<2]=0.1286\).
  8. (ANS=1) First, \(0.9 \cdot 70 = 63\). An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 47 excludes 63 measurements. So, \(\text{prop}[|\mathbf{x}-47|<1]=0.9\).
