01_data_basics/02_proportion/02_list_proportion_between_v1

The lengths (in centimeters) of 10 lizards were recorded.

\[\mathbf{x} = 58, 59, 54, 55, 53, 56, 56, 51, 53, 57 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): lizard_data.csv


  1. What proportion of the measurements are inside 51.5 cm to 52.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}>51.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<52.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[51.5<\mathbf{x}<52.5]\).
  2. What proportion of the measurements are outside 51.5 cm to 53.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\mathbf{x}<51.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>53.5]\). In other words, determine \(\text{prop}[\texttt{NOT}(51.5<\mathbf{x}<53.5)]\).
  3. What proportion of the measurements are closer than 0.5 cm from 55 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55\big|<0.5\right]\).
  4. What proportion of the measurements are farther than 2 cm from 54.5 cm? In other words, determine \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54.5\big|>2\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 54 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|<r\right] ~=~ 0.1\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 54 cm such that \(\text{prop}\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|>r\right] ~=~ 0.2\).
