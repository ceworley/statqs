We can visualize the “68-96-100” rule with a density curve. Notice the area of each region is shown, and you can estimate the areas by counting percentage boxes.


Population \(X\) has measurements that are normally distributed with mean \(\mu=39\) and standard deviation \(\sigma=9\). Use the empirical rule (68-96-100 rule) to answer the following questions.


  1. Evaluate \(\text{prop}[X<39]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\text{prop}[X>57]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\text{prop}[|X-39|<18]\).
  4. Determine boundary \(b\) such that \(\text{prop}[X<b]=0.02\).
  5. Determine boundary \(b\) such that \(\text{prop}[X>b]=0.84\).
  6. Determine radius \(r\) such that \(\text{prop}[|X-39|<r]=0.68\).
