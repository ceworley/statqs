When measuring individuals from a population, we expect most measurements to be within the interval of typical measurements. We will define the interval of typical measurements (using interval notation): \[\text{interval of typical measurements} = (\mu-2\sigma,\,\mu+2\sigma) \] In other words, we expect we expect most measurements to be between two bounds. \[\text{lower bound of interval of typical measurements} = \mu-2\sigma \] \[\text{upper bound of interval of typical measurements} = \mu+2\sigma \]

Actual Question

A population of lizards has a mean length of \(\mu = 78.3\) cm and a standard deviation of \(\sigma=2.7\) cm. Determine the interval of typical measurements.


  1. Determine the lower bound of the interval of typical measurements.
  2. Determine the upper bound of the interval of typical measurements.
