The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=51) There are 51 measurements less than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=51\).
  2. (ANS=28) There are 28 measurements more than 5.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>5.5]=28\).
  3. (ANS=37) There are 37 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=37\).
  4. (ANS=84) There are 84 measurements further than 0.5 units from 5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>0.5]=84\).
  5. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 56 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=56\).
  6. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 65 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=65\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 5 contains 32 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|<1.5]=32\).
  8. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 5.5 excludes 60 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5.5|>2]=60\).
