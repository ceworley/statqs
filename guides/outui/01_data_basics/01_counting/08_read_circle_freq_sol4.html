The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=54) There are 54 measurements less than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<3.5]=54\).
  2. (ANS=62) There are 62 measurements more than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=62\).
  3. (ANS=49) There are 49 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=49\).
  4. (ANS=51) There are 51 measurements further than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|>1.5]=51\).
  5. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 71 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=71\).
  6. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 62 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>2.5]=62\).
  7. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 3 contains 16 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=16\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 excludes 47 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=47\).
