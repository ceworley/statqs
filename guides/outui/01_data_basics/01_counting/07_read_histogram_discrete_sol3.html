The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=29) There are 29 measurements less than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=29\).
  2. (ANS=40) There are 40 measurements more than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=40\).
  3. (ANS=13) There are 13 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 2. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2|<0.5]=13\).
  4. (ANS=80) There are 80 measurements further than 0.5 units from 6. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-6|>0.5]=80\).
  5. (ANS=5.5) A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 60 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=60\).
  6. (ANS=5.5) A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 30 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>5.5]=30\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 4.5 contains 22 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4.5|<1]=22\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 2 excludes 77 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2|>0.5]=77\).
