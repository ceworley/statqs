The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=9) There are 9 measurements less than 47.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<47.5]=9\).
  2. (ANS=42) There are 42 measurements more than 49. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>49]=42\).
  3. (ANS=13) There are 13 measurements closer than 1 units from 47. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-47|<1]=13\).
  4. (ANS=61) There are 61 measurements further than 0.25 units from 49.25. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-49.25|>0.25]=61\).
  5. (ANS=47) A boundary at \(b=47\) has 6 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<47]=6\).
  6. (ANS=47.5) A boundary at \(b=47.5\) has 61 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>47.5]=61\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 48.5 contains 28 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-48.5|<1]=28\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 47 excludes 51 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-47|>1.5]=51\).
