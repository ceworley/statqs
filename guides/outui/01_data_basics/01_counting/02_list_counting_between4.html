01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v4

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 47, 42, 47, 47, 41, 42, 43, 43, 46, 46 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 43.5 to 47.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>43.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<47.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[43.5<\mathbf{x}<47.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 43.5 to 48.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<43.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>48.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(43.5<\mathbf{x}<48.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 1 unit from 47.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-47.5\big|<1\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 2.5 units from 45? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-45\big|>2.5\right]\).
  5. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 46 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-46\big|<r\right] ~=~ 2\).
  6. Determine a half-integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 43 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-43\big|>r\right] ~=~ 6\).
