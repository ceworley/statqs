The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=56) There are 56 measurements less than 54. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<54]=56\).
  2. (ANS=53) There are 53 measurements more than 56. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>56]=53\).
  3. (ANS=11) There are 11 measurements closer than 1 units from 55. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-55|<1]=11\).
  4. (ANS=118) There are 118 measurements further than 1 units from 57. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-57|>1]=118\).
  5. (ANS=54) A boundary at \(b=54\) has 56 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<54]=56\).
  6. (ANS=56) A boundary at \(b=56\) has 53 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>56]=53\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 53 contains 6 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-53|<1]=6\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 57 excludes 118 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-57|>1]=118\).
