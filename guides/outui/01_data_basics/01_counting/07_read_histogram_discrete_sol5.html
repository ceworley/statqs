The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=17) There are 17 measurements less than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=17\).
  2. (ANS=23) There are 23 measurements more than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=23\).
  3. (ANS=46) There are 46 measurements closer than 2 units from 3.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<2]=46\).
  4. (ANS=34) There are 34 measurements further than 1.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=34\).
  5. (ANS=5.5) A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 57 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=57\).
  6. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 23 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=23\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 4.5 contains 26 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4.5|<1]=26\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 5 excludes 60 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-5|>0.5]=60\).
