The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=33) There are 33 measurements less than 10.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<10.5]=33\).
  2. (ANS=7) There are 7 measurements more than 10.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>10.5]=7\).
  3. (ANS=32) There are 32 measurements closer than 5 units from 6.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-6.5|<5]=32\).
  4. (ANS=20) There are 20 measurements further than 3 units from 7.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-7.5|>3]=20\).
  5. (ANS=10.5) A boundary at \(b=10.5\) has 33 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<10.5]=33\).
  6. (ANS=4.5) A boundary at \(b=4.5\) has 27 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=27\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 9 contains 10 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-9|<1.5]=10\).
  8. (ANS=3) An interval with radius \(r=3\) around center 8.5 excludes 21 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-8.5|>3]=21\).
