01_data_basics/01_counting/01_list_counting_basic_v4

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 59, 57, 53, 59, 57, 53, 56, 57, 59, 56 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are less than 59? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<59]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 59? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le59]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are greater than 57? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>57]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 53? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge53]\).
  5. If 7 of the measurements are less than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 7\).
  6. If 4 of the measurements are less than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 4\).
  7. If 8 of the measurements are greater than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 8\).
  8. If 6 of the measurements are greater than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 6\).
