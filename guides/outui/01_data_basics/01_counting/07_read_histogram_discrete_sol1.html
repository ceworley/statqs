The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=17) There are 17 measurements less than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=17\).
  2. (ANS=8) There are 8 measurements more than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=8\).
  3. (ANS=20) There are 20 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=20\).
  4. (ANS=34) There are 34 measurements further than 0.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|>0.5]=34\).
  5. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 17 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=17\).
  6. (ANS=3.5) A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 14 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=14\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 2.5 contains 16 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|<1]=16\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 4 excludes 20 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|>1.5]=20\).
