  1. (ANS=5) There are 5 measurements between 43.5 and 47.5. \[\#[43.5<\mathbf{x}<47.5]=5\]
  2. (ANS=5) There are 5 measurements outside 43.5 and 48.5. \[\#[\mathbf{x}<43.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>48.5]=5\]
  3. (ANS=3) There are 3 measurements closer than 1 units from 47.5. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are between 46.5 and 48.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 1 from 47.5 and adding 1 to 47.5. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-47.5\big|<1\right]=3\]
  4. (ANS=3) There are 3 measurements farther than 2.5 units from 45. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are outside 42.5 and 47.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 2.5 from 45 and adding 2.5 to 45. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-45\big|>2.5\right]=3\]
  5. (ANS=0.5) You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 0.5\) satisfies the equation, because \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-46\big|<0.5\right] ~=~ 2\). Notice the boundaries are 45.5 and 46.5, which are found by adding/subtracting 0.5 to/from 46. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-46\big|<0.5\right] ~=~ 2\]
  6. (ANS=1.5) You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 1.5\) satisfies the equation, because \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-43\big|>1.5\right] ~=~ 6\). Notice the boundaries are 41.5 and 44.5, which are found by adding/subtracting 1.5 to/from 43. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-43\big|>1.5\right] ~=~ 6\]
