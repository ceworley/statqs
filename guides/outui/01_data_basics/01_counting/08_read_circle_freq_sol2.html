The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=59) There are 59 measurements less than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<4.5]=59\).
  2. (ANS=86) There are 86 measurements more than 1.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=86\).
  3. (ANS=43) There are 43 measurements closer than 1.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<1.5]=43\).
  4. (ANS=86) There are 86 measurements further than 0.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|>0.5]=86\).
  5. (ANS=2.5) A boundary at \(b=2.5\) has 33 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=33\).
  6. (ANS=3.5) A boundary at \(b=3.5\) has 55 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=55\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 2.5 contains 31 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2.5|<1]=31\).
  8. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 3.5 excludes 38 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|>2]=38\).
