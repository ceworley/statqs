The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=9) There are 9 measurements less than 32.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<32.5]=9\).
  2. (ANS=117) There are 117 measurements more than 32. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>32]=117\).
  3. (ANS=15) There are 15 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 32.5. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32.5|<0.5]=15\).
  4. (ANS=106) There are 106 measurements further than 0.25 units from 33.75. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-33.75|>0.25]=106\).
  5. (ANS=32) A boundary at \(b=32\) has 3 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<32]=3\).
  6. (ANS=34) A boundary at \(b=34\) has 78 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>34]=78\).
  7. (ANS=0.75) An interval with radius \(r=0.75\) around center 32.25 contains 17 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32.25|<0.75]=17\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 32 excludes 103 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-32|>1]=103\).
