01_data_basics/01_counting/02_list_counting_between_v2

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 55, 53, 51, 53, 53, 54, 52, 53, 52, 52 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_between_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are inside 54.5 to 57.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>54.5\texttt{ AND } \mathbf{x}<57.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[54.5<\mathbf{x}<57.5]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are outside 55.5 to 56.5? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<55.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>56.5]\). In other words, determine \(\#[\texttt{NOT}(55.5<\mathbf{x}<56.5)]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are closer than 1 unit from 52.5? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-52.5\big|<1\right]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are farther than 1.5 units from 54? In other words, determine \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|>1.5\right]\).
  5. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 56.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|<r\right] ~=~ 2\).
  6. Determine an integer radius \(r\) of an interval with center 56.5 such that \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|>r\right] ~=~ 8\).
