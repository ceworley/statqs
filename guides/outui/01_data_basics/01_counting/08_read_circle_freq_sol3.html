The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=85) There are 85 measurements less than 7.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=85\).
  2. (ANS=55) There are 55 measurements more than 4.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>4.5]=55\).
  3. (ANS=14) There are 14 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|<0.5]=14\).
  4. (ANS=91) There are 91 measurements further than 0.5 units from 2. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-2|>0.5]=91\).
  5. (ANS=7.5) A boundary at \(b=7.5\) has 85 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<7.5]=85\).
  6. (ANS=1.5) A boundary at \(b=1.5\) has 90 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=90\).
  7. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 contains 35 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<1.5]=35\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 excludes 65 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=65\).
