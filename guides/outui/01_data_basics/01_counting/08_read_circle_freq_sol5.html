The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=34) There are 34 measurements less than 2.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<2.5]=34\).
  2. (ANS=47) There are 47 measurements more than 3.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>3.5]=47\).
  3. (ANS=19) There are 19 measurements closer than 0.5 units from 3. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|<0.5]=19\).
  4. (ANS=82) There are 82 measurements further than 0.5 units from 4. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-4|>0.5]=82\).
  5. (ANS=5.5) A boundary at \(b=5.5\) has 86 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<5.5]=86\).
  6. (ANS=1.5) A boundary at \(b=1.5\) has 83 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>1.5]=83\).
  7. (ANS=2) An interval with radius \(r=2\) around center 3.5 contains 69 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3.5|<2]=69\).
  8. (ANS=1.5) An interval with radius \(r=1.5\) around center 3 excludes 46 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-3|>1.5]=46\).
