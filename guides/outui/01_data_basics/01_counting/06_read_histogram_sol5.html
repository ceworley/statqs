The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=35) There are 35 measurements less than 42. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<42]=35\).
  2. (ANS=84) There are 84 measurements more than 44. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>44]=84\).
  3. (ANS=27) There are 27 measurements closer than 3 units from 39. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-39|<3]=27\).
  4. (ANS=123) There are 123 measurements further than 2 units from 34. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-34|>2]=123\).
  5. (ANS=34) A boundary at \(b=34\) has 4 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<34]=4\).
  6. (ANS=36) A boundary at \(b=36\) has 122 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>36]=122\).
  7. (ANS=6) An interval with radius \(r=6\) around center 40 contains 58 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-40|<6]=58\).
  8. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 47 excludes 108 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-47|>1]=108\).
