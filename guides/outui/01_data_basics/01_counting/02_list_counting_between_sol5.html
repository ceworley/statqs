  1. (ANS=9) There are 9 measurements between 50.5 and 58.5. \[\#[50.5<\mathbf{x}<58.5]=9\]
  2. (ANS=8) There are 8 measurements outside 54.5 and 55.5. \[\#[\mathbf{x}<54.5\texttt{ OR } \mathbf{x}>55.5]=8\]
  3. (ANS=10) There are 10 measurements closer than 4 units from 55.5. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are between 51.5 and 59.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 4 from 55.5 and adding 4 to 55.5. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-55.5\big|<4\right]=10\]
  4. (ANS=1) There are 1 measurements farther than 3.5 units from 56. It might help to rephrase the problem as finding how many measurements are outside 52.5 and 59.5. These boundaries are found by subtracting 3.5 from 56 and adding 3.5 to 56. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56\big|>3.5\right]=1\]
  5. (ANS=1.5) You can guess and check until you find a half-integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 1.5\) satisfies the equation, because \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|<1.5\right] ~=~ 5\). Notice the boundaries are 52.5 and 55.5, which are found by adding/subtracting 1.5 to/from 54. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-54\big|<1.5\right] ~=~ 5\]
  6. (ANS=2) You can guess and check until you find an integer that satisfies the equation. It turns out that \(r = 2\) satisfies the equation, because \(\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|>2\right] ~=~ 5\). Notice the boundaries are 54.5 and 58.5, which are found by adding/subtracting 2 to/from 56.5. \[\#\left[\big|\mathbf{x}-56.5\big|>2\right] ~=~ 5\]
