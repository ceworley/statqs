01_data_basics/01_counting/01_list_counting_basic_v1

A random procedure generated the following sample (sequence of measurements):

\[\mathbf{x} = 96, 92, 96, 90, 94, 90, 91, 91, 94, 93 \]

You can download the data as a CSV file (for importing into a spreadsheet or R): list_counting_basic_data.csv


  1. How many of the measurements are less than 96? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}<96]\).
  2. How many of the measurements are less than or equal to 93? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le93]\).
  3. How many of the measurements are greater than 96? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}>96]\).
  4. How many of the measurements are greater than or equal to 90? In other words, determine \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge90]\).
  5. If 0 of the measurements are less than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<x_{\text{c}}] = 0\).
  6. If 5 of the measurements are less than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\le x_{\text{c}}] = 5\).
  7. If 2 of the measurements are greater than \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>x_{\text{c}}] = 2\).
  8. If 8 of the measurements are greater than or equal to \(x_{\text{c}}\), and \(x_{\text{c}}\) is a measurement from sample \(\mathbf{x}\), then what is \(x_{\text{c}}\)? In other words, determine a measurement \(x_{\text{c}}\) from sequence \(\mathbf{x}\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}\ge x_{\text{c}}] = 8\).
