The first 4 questions involve adding up frequencies of the indicated intervals. The last 4 questions can be done by guessing and checking until something works.


  1. (ANS=21) There are 21 measurements less than 62.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<62.5]=21\).
  2. (ANS=99) There are 99 measurements more than 62.5. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>62.5]=99\).
  3. (ANS=29) There are 29 measurements closer than 1 units from 62. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-62|<1]=29\).
  4. (ANS=109) There are 109 measurements further than 0.25 units from 62.75. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-62.75|>0.25]=109\).
  5. (ANS=64) A boundary at \(b=64\) has 48 measurements less than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}<64]=48\).
  6. (ANS=62) A boundary at \(b=62\) has 104 measurements more than it. So, \(\#[\mathbf{x}>62]=104\).
  7. (ANS=1) An interval with radius \(r=1\) around center 63.5 contains 52 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63.5|<1]=52\).
  8. (ANS=0.5) An interval with radius \(r=0.5\) around center 63.5 excludes 104 measurements. So, \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-63.5|>0.5]=104\).
