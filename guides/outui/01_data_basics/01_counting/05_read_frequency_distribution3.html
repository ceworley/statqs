01_data_basics/01_counting/05_read_frequency_distribution_v3

A random procedure generated 120 measurements, which were organized into the frequency distribution shown below. You can assume the measurements are of a continuous random variable, such that every measurement is in one of the intervals (and not on a break).

interval frequency
65 — 70 14
70 — 75 21
75 — 80 21
80 — 85 18
85 — 90 23
90 — 95 23

  1. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}<85]\).
  2. Evaluate \(\#[\mathbf{x}>70]\).
  3. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|<5]\).
  4. Evaluate \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|>2.5]\).
  5. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}<b] = 56\).
  6. Find boundary \(b\) such that \(\#[\mathbf{x}>b] = 64\).
  7. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-82.5|<r] = 62\).
  8. Find radius \(r\) such that \(\#[|\mathbf{x}-80|>r] = 37\).
