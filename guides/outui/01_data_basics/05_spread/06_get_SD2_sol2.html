First, determine the mean of the sample.

\[\bar{x} = \frac{\sum \mathbf{x}}{n} =48 \]

Determine the squared deviations (squared distances from \(\bar{x}\)).

\[i\] \[\mathbf{x}\] deviations = \(\mathbf{x}-\bar{x}\) SqrDev = \((\mathbf{x}-\bar{x})^2\)

Determine the unbiased sample variance by summing the squared deviations and dividing the sum by \(n-1\).

\[s^2 = \frac{\sum (\mathbf{x}-\bar{x})^2}{n-1} = \frac{88}{8-1} = 12.5714286 \]

Determine the corrected sample standard deviation by taking the square root of the unbiased sample variance.

\[s=\sqrt{s^2}=\sqrt{12.5714286}=3.545621\]

You could also do it the long way.

x = read.csv("get_SD.csv")$x
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
deviations = x-xbar
sqrdev = deviations^2
VAR = sum(sqrdev)/(n-1)
SD = sqrt(VAR)
