The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt}]=\frac{136}{695}=0.196\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt or green}]=\frac{136+131-12}{695}=0.367\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given quilt}]=\frac{14}{136}=0.103\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given dog}] = 0.232\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given quilt}] = 0.103\), so a dog is more likely to be blue than a quilt is. / .
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{kite and indigo}]=\frac{35}{695}=0.0504\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{green}]=\frac{131}{695}=0.188\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt given blue}]=\frac{14}{135}=0.104\)
