This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 6 from a set of size 9.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 9 \\\\ r &= 6 \\\\ {_{9}C_{6}} &= \frac{9!}{(9-6)!\cdot 6! } \\\\ &= \frac{9!}{3! \cdot 6!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4}{ 6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 84 \end{aligned}\]
