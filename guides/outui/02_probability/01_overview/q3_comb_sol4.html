This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 4 from a set of size 12.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 12 \\\\ r &= 4 \\\\ {_{12}C_{4}} &= \frac{12!}{(12-4)!\cdot 4! } \\\\ &= \frac{12!}{8! \cdot 4!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 12\cdot11\cdot10\cdot9}{ 4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 495 \end{aligned}\]
