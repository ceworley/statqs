The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{bike or indigo}]=\frac{168+126-13}{712}=0.395\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{white}]=\frac{150}{712}=0.211\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{dog given blue}]=\frac{22}{142}=0.155\)
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{needle}]=\frac{139}{712}=0.195\)
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{shovel and white}]=\frac{34}{712}=0.0478\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given bike}] = 0.0774\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{indigo given needle}] = 0.209\), so a needle is more likely to be indigo than a bike is. / .
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given mop}]=\frac{42}{161}=0.261\)
