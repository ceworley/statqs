The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue}]=\frac{107}{462}=0.232\)
  2. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{shovel and blue}]=\frac{24}{462}=0.0519\)
  3. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given rug}] = 0.257\) and \(\mathbb{P}[\text{blue given shovel}] = 0.183\), so a rug is more likely to be blue than a shovel is. / .
  4. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt given blue}]=\frac{45}{107}=0.421\)
  5. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{rug or pink}]=\frac{148+119-42}{462}=0.487\)
  6. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given quilt}]=\frac{50}{183}=0.273\)
  7. \(\mathbb{P}[\text{quilt}]=\frac{183}{462}=0.396\)

ANSWERS: 0.232, 0.0519, c(TRUE, FALSE), 0.421, 0.487, 0.273, 0.396
