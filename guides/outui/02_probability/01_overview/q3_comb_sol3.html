This scenario describes a combinations problem (order does not matter). We are considering the subsets of size 5 from a set of size 12.

\[\begin{aligned} {_nC_r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)! \cdot r!} \\\\ n &= 12 \\\\ r &= 5 \\\\ {_{12}C_{5}} &= \frac{12!}{(12-5)!\cdot 5! } \\\\ &= \frac{12!}{7! \cdot 5!} \\\\ &= \frac{ 12\cdot11\cdot10\cdot9\cdot8}{ 5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1} \\\\ &= 792 \end{aligned}\]


ANSWERS: 792
