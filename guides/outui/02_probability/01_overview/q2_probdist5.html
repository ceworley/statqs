The spinner’s probability distribution is shown below.

It can also be downloaded as a csv.


  1. What is the probability of spinning 4? In other words, what is \(\mathbb{P}[X=4]\)?
  2. What is the probability of spinning 1 or 6? In other words, what is \(\mathbb{P}[X=1 ~\text{ or }~ X=6]\)?
  3. If spinning twice, what is the probability of first spinning 1 and then spinning 6? In other words, what is \(\mathbb{P}[X_1=1 ~\text{ and }~ X_2=6]\)?
  4. What is the probability of spinning at most 3? In other words, what is \(\mathbb{P}[X\le 3]\)?
  5. Determine the mean of the probability distribution by using \(\mu = \sum x\cdot \mathbb{P}[x]\).
  6. Determine the standard deviation of the probability distribution by using \(\sigma = \sqrt{\sum (x-\mu)^2 \cdot \mathbb{P}[x]}\).
