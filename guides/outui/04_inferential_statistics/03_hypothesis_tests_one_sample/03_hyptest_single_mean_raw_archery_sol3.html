Determine the sample statistics (\(\bar{x}\) and \(s\)). I would recommend using a spreadsheet or R (or another computer-based method). \[\bar{x} = -7.262\] \[s = 45.18 \] Evaluate the \(t\) statistic.

\[t = \frac{|-7.262-0|}{(45.18)/\sqrt{80}} = 1.44 \] Restate the \(p\)-value. \[p\text{-value} = P\left(|T|>1.44\right) \] Remember, the degree of freedom is one less than the sample size. \[\text{df} = 79 \]

At this point, there are various ways to determine the \(p\)-value. The least accurate way is to use the \(t\)-table. Go to the row with \(\text{df}=n-1=80-1=79\).


If we go to the row with df=79, we can see that our calculated \(t\), 1.44, is between 1.29 and 1.66. Thus, we know \(P(|T|>1.44)\) is between 0.1 and 0.2. You could get pretty close by using linear interpolation. \[P(|T|>1.44)\approx \left(\frac{1.44-1.29}{1.66-1.29}\right)\left(0.1-0.2\right)+0.2 = 0.159\]
