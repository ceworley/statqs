Analysis

To get the \(p\)-value: \[p\text{-value} ~=~ P\left(\big|T\big| > \frac{\big|\bar{x}-\mu_0\big|}{s/\sqrt{n}} \right) \]

In this situation, the null population mean is zero. \[\mu_0=0\] In other words, the null hypothesis claims the sight is correct and the difference between \(\bar{x}\) and 0 is just due to chance. The alternative hypothesis claims the sight needs to be adjusted: the difference between \(\bar{x}\) and 0 is partly because the sight is off.

Determine the \(p\)-value.

Archie will adjust her sight if the \(p\)-value is less than 0.05, because this indicates there is statistical significance. Does Archie adjust her sight?
