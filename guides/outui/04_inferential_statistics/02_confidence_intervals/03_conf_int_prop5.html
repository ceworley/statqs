In \(n=104\) trials, there were \(n_\text{s}=40\) successes, so the sample proportion is \(\hat{p}=\frac{40}{104}=0.3846\). You are tasked with determining the confidence interval (of the population proportion) with confidence level \(\gamma=0.76\).

To do this, you first determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(|Z|<z^\star)=0.76\). Then, evaluate \(\hat{p}\pm z^\star\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}\) to determine the boundaries of the confidence interval.

(You can round answers to the hundredths place.)


  1. Determine the lower boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\hat{p}- z^\star\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}\).
  2. Determine the upper boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\hat{p}+ z^\star\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}\).
