x = c(669, 662, 683, 672, 662, 670, 676, 685, 689, 688, 669, 679, 673, 664, 703)
n = length(x)
xbar = mean(x)
s = sd(x)
SE = s/sqrt(n)
gamma = 0.8   # Probability T is between -tstar and tstar
cumulative = gamma+(1-gamma)/2   # Probability T is less than tstar
tstar = qt(cumulative, n-1)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
print(data.frame(n,xbar,s,SE,tstar,ME,LB,UB,row.names=""))

  1. The sample size \(=n=15\).
  2. The sample mean \(=\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x}{n}=676.27\).
  3. The sample standard deviation \(=s=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}=11.57\).
  4. The estimated standard error \(=\text{SE}=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{11.57}{\sqrt{15}}=2.99\).
  5. You can show, when \(n=15\), then \(\text{df}=14\), and \(P(|T|<1.35)=0.8\), so the boundary \(=t^\star=1.35\).
  6. The estimated margin of error \(=\text{ME}=\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}=\frac{(1.35)(11.57)}{\sqrt{15}}=4.02\).
  7. The lower boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{LB}=\bar{x}-\text{ME}=676.27-4.02=672.25\).
  8. The upper boundary of the confidence interval \(=\text{UB}=\bar{x}+\text{ME}=676.27+4.02=680.29\).
