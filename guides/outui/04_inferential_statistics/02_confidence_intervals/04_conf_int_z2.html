To do this, you need to first determine \(z^\star\) such that \(P(|Z|<z^\star)=0.65\). Then, the boundaries are determined by evaluating \(\bar{x}\pm \frac{z^\star \sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\).

(You can round \(z^\star\) to the nearest hundredth and the boundaries to the nearest tenth.)


  1. Determine the lower boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\bar{x}-\frac{z^\star \sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\).
  2. Determine the upper boundary of the confidence interval by using \(\bar{x}+\frac{z^\star \sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\).
