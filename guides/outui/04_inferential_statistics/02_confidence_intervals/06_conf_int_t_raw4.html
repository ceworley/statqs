A sample was taken from a population. The measurements are shown below and can be downloaded as a csv.

544, 567, 563, 548, 521, 467, 472, 461, 552, 592, 593, 530, 465, 444, 474, 495, 534, 501, 514, 582, 570, 592, 460, 630, 592, 547, 559, 539, 511, 508, 499, 580, 525, 505, 525, 595, 476, 485, 494, 522, 568, 569, 553

You are tasked with determining the confidence interval (of the population mean) with the confidence level of \(\gamma=0.96\).


  1. Determine \(n\), the sample size.
  2. Determine \(\bar{x}\), the sample mean.
  3. Determine \(s\), the sample standard deviation (with Bessel correction).
  4. Calculate \(\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\), the estimated standard error.
  5. Determine \(t^\star\) such that \(P(|T|<t^\star)=0.96\). Remember, \(\text{df}=n-1\).
  6. Calculate \(\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}\), the estimated margin of error.
  7. Calculate \(\bar{x}-\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}\), the lower boundary of the 96% confidence interval.
  8. Calculate \(\bar{x}+\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}\), the upper boundary of the 96% confidence interval.
