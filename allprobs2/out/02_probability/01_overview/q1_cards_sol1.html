The key logical terms are “and”, “or”, and “given”. Notice that I am using “given” as a shorter version of “under the condition”.


  1. ℙ[bike given pink]=1658=0.276\mathbb{P}[\text{bike given pink}]=\frac{16}{58}=0.276
  2. ℙ[pink given bike]=0.11\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given bike}] = 0.11 and ℙ[pink given quilt]=0.216\mathbb{P}[\text{pink given quilt}] = 0.216, so a quilt is more likely to be pink than a bike is. / .
  3. ℙ[pink]=58415=0.14\mathbb{P}[\text{pink}]=\frac{58}{415}=0.14
  4. ℙ[cat]=159415=0.383\mathbb{P}[\text{cat}]=\frac{159}{415}=0.383
  5. ℙ[orange given bike]=30145=0.207\mathbb{P}[\text{orange given bike}]=\frac{30}{145}=0.207
  6. ℙ[cat and violet]=45415=0.108\mathbb{P}[\text{cat and violet}]=\frac{45}{415}=0.108
  7. ℙ[cat or violet]=159+76−45415=0.458\mathbb{P}[\text{cat or violet}]=\frac{159+76-45}{415}=0.458
