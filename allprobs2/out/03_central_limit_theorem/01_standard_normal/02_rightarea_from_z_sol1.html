First, you need to identify that we are looking for a right area. This is because large values of ZZ satisfy Z>−0.61Z>-0.61.


By using the z-table, we find the left area (even though we eventually want the right area).

P(Z<−0.61)=0.2709P(Z<-0.61)=0.2709

We use the rule of compliments to determine the right area.

P(Z>−0.61)=1−P(Z<−0.61)=1−0.2709=0.7291\begin{aligned} P(Z > -0.61) &= 1-P(Z < -0.61) \\ &= 1-0.2709 \\ &= 0.7291 \end{aligned}

Method 2: You might also recognize that the normal distribution is symmetric. Thus,

P(Z>−0.61)=P(Z<0.61)=0.7291\begin{aligned} P(Z > -0.61) &= P(Z < 0.61) \\ &= 0.7291 \end{aligned}
