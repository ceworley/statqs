We calculate the mean and standard deviation of the random sum using the formulas from the central limit theorem. μ∑X=nμX=(256)(2.5)=640\begin{aligned} \mu_{ \sum X} &= n\mu_{ X} \\ &= (256)(2.5) \\ &= 640 \end{aligned}

σ∑X=σXn=(1.118034)256=17.8885438\begin{aligned} \sigma_{ \sum X} &= \sigma_{ X} \sqrt{n} \\ &= (1.118034)\sqrt{256} \\ &= 17.8885438 \end{aligned}

The central limit theorem tells us that the random sum is approximately normal with the parameters calculated above.

∑X∼N(640,17.8885438)\sum X \sim N(640,\,17.8885438)

In other words, we can approximate the summing of 256 rolls of 4-sided dice with a single spin of the following spinner.
