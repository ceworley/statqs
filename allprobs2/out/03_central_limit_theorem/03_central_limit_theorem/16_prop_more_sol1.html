We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.57\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.57 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.57)(0.43)n=0.0502673\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.57)(0.43)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0502673

We determine a zz-score. (The common notation is p̂\hat{p} for a specific proportion.) z=p̂−μσ=0.5412−0.570.0502673=−0.5729368\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.5412-0.57}{0.0502673} \\\\ &= -0.5729368 \end{aligned}

Then, find the standard normal probability.

P(Z>−0.5729368)=0.7166562P(Z > -0.5729368) ~=~ 0.7166562
