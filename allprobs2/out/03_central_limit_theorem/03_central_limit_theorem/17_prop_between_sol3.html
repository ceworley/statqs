We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.33\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.33 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.33)(0.67)169=0.0361702\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.33)(0.67)}{169}} ~=~ 0.0361702

We determine both zz-scores. We get the first zz-score.

z1=p̂1−μσ=0.2988−0.330.0361702=−0.8621303\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{\hat{p}_1 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2988-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= -0.8621303 \end{aligned}

We get the second zz-score.

z2=p̂2−μσ=0.358−0.330.0361702=0.7737905\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{\hat{p}_2 - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.358-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= 0.7737905 \end{aligned}

Then, find the standard normal probability.

P(−0.8621303<Z<0.7737905)=0.5861647P(-0.8621303 < Z < 0.7737905) ~=~ 0.5861647
