We determine the mean and standard deviation of the proportion sampling distribution. μ=p=0.33\mu ~=~ p ~=~ 0.33 σ=p(1−p)n=(0.33)(0.67)n=0.0361702\sigma ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}} ~=~ \sqrt{\frac{(0.33)(0.67)}{n}} ~=~ 0.0361702

We determine a zz-score. (The common notation is p̂\hat{p} for a specific proportion.) z=p̂−μσ=0.2988−0.330.0361702=−0.8625883\begin{aligned} z &= \frac{\hat{p} - \mu}{\sigma} \\\\ &= \frac{0.2988-0.33}{0.0361702} \\\\ &= -0.8625883 \end{aligned}

Then, find the standard normal probability.

P(Z<−0.8625883)=0.1941819P(Z < -0.8625883) ~=~ 0.1941819
