First, draw a sketch. We can label the CC axis by adding integer multiples of 150 to 480. We know to shade the right because high values of CC satisfy the condition C>cC>c (regardless of the exact value of cc). We don’t know exactly where to place the boundary, but we know the right area is 0.73.


We know how to find the left area. P(C<c)=1−P(C>c)=1−0.73=0.27\begin{aligned} P(C<c) &= 1-P(C>c) \\ &= 1-0.73 \\ &= 0.27 \end{aligned}


As an intermediate step, we find zz such that P(Z<z)=0.27P(Z<z)=0.27.

We now convert the zz score into a cc score. c=μ+zσ=480+(−0.6128)(150)=388.1\begin{aligned} c &= \mu+z\sigma \\ &= 480+(-0.6128)(150) \\ &= 388.1 \end{aligned}
