First, draw a sketch. We can label the JJ axis by adding integer multiples of 30 to 170. We know to shade the two tails because values far from 170 satisfy the condition |J−170|>30\big|J-170\big| > 30. We draw the boundaries at 170−30=140170-30=140 and 170+30=200170+30=200 because those are the solutions to |J−170|=30\big|J-170\big| = 30. We can also rephrase the probability.

P(|J−170|>30)=P(J<140 OR J>200)P\left(\big|J-170\big| > 30\right) ~=~ P\big(J<140 \text{ OR } J>200\big)


We calculate the zz values of the boundaries. Left boundary: z1=140−17030=−3030=−1\begin{aligned} z_1 &= \frac{140-170}{30} \\\\ &= \frac{-30}{30} \\\\ &= -1 \end{aligned}

Right boundary:

z2=200−17030=3030=1\begin{aligned} z_2 &= \frac{200-170}{30} \\\\ &= \frac{30}{30} \\\\ &= 1 \end{aligned}

We have rephrased our problem into a standard normal probability problem, because

P(|J−170|>30)=P(|Z|>1)P\left(\big|J-170\big| > 30\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big|>1\right)

So, we just need to evaluate P(|Z|>1)P(|Z|>1). I will also point out that 3030=1\frac{30}{30} = 1. In general, if XX is normally distributed, then: P(|X−μ|>d)=P(|Z|>dσ)P\left(\big|X-\mu\big|>d\right) ~=~ P\left(\big|Z\big| > \frac{d}{\sigma}\right)

From here we have a formula that lets us use the zz table. (We practiced this part before.) P(|Z|>1)=2−2×P(Z<1)=2−2×0.8413=0.3174\begin{aligned} P(|Z|>1) &= 2- 2 \times P(Z<1) \\ &= 2 - 2 \times 0.8413 \\ &= 0.3174 \end{aligned}
