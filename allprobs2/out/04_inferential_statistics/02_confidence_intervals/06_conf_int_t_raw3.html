A sample was taken from a population. The measurements are shown below and can be downloaded as a csv.

282, 312, 279, 315, 301, 307, 340, 323, 277, 212, 246, 334, 356, 329

You are tasked with determining the confidence interval (of the population mean) with the confidence level of γ=0.8\gamma=0.8.


  1. Determine nn, the sample size.
  2. Determine x‾\bar{x}, the sample mean.
  3. Determine ss, the sample standard deviation (with Bessel correction).
  4. Calculate sn\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}, the estimated standard error.
  5. Determine t⋆t^\star such that P(|T|<t⋆)=0.8P(|T|<t^\star)=0.8. Remember, df=n−1\text{df}=n-1.
  6. Calculate t⋆sn\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the estimated margin of error.
  7. Calculate x‾−t⋆sn\bar{x}-\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the lower boundary of the 80% confidence interval.
  8. Calculate x‾+t⋆sn\bar{x}+\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}, the upper boundary of the 80% confidence interval.
