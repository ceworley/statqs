First, determine t⋆t^\star such that P(|T|<t⋆)=0.96P(|T|<t^\star)=0.96. It can help to draw a sketch.


The entire area under the density curve is 1. Thus, we can determine the areas of the tails (using symmetry). area of each tail=1−γ2=1−0.962=0.02\text{area of each tail} = \frac{1-\gamma}{2} = \frac{1-0.96}{2} = 0.02


Thus, we determine a left area. P(T<t⋆)=γ+1−γ2=0.96+0.02=0.98P(T<t^\star)=\gamma + \frac{1-\gamma}{2}= 0.96+0.02=0.98 It should be mentioned the expression can be simplified. P(T<t⋆)=12+γ2=0.5+0.48=0.98P(T<t^\star)= \frac{1}{2}+\frac{\gamma}{2}= 0.5+0.48=0.98


We rephrase the puzzle. We wish to determine t⋆t^\star such that P(T<t⋆)=0.98P(T<t^\star)=0.98. This is easy to determine using a tt-table, qt in R, t.inv in spreadsheets, or other methods for evaluating the quantile function.

P(T<2.25)=0.98P(T<2.25)=0.98 In the original phrasing,

P(|T|<2.25)=0.96P(|T|<2.25)=0.96

So, t⋆=2.25t^\star = 2.25. Now, use the given expressions.

Lower bound=25.1−(2.25)(7.6)/16=20.8\text{Lower bound} = 25.1-(2.25)(7.6)/\sqrt{16} ~=~ 20.8 Upper bound=25.1+(2.25)(7.6)/16=29.4\text{Upper bound} = 25.1+(2.25)(7.6)/\sqrt{16} ~=~ 29.4

Spreadsheet

A spreadsheet can use the confidence.t function. It takes three arguments:

  • Alpha (α\alpha) is the two-tail area, α=1−γ=1−0.96=0.04\alpha=1-\gamma=1-0.96=0.04
  • sample standard deviation, s=7.6s=7.6
  • Sample size, n=16n=16

The function returns the margin of error. margin of error=t⋆sn\text{margin of error} = \frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}

So, in this case, if in a spreadsheet you typed =confidence.t(1-0.96,7.6,16), the result would be the margin of error, 4.2722266. You then add/subtract the margin of error to/from the sample mean. x‾−ME=25.1−4.272=20.828\bar{x}-\text{ME} = 25.1-4.272 = 20.828 x‾+ME=25.1+4.272=29.372\bar{x}+\text{ME} = 25.1+4.272 = 29.372

You can do this with a spreadsheet.


R

xbar = 25.1
s = 7.6
n = 16
gamma = 0.96
df = n-1   # df is the degrees of freedom
tstar = qt(0.5+gamma/2, df)
ME = tstar*s/sqrt(n)
LB = xbar-ME
UB = xbar+ME
cat(sprintf("The lower bound: %.4f\nThe upper bound: %.4f",LB,UB))
