A population’s mean (μ\mu) and standard deviation (σ\sigma) are unknown, but the population is approximately normal. A sample of size n=10n=10 is taken, and the sample mean is calculated: x‾=26.6\bar{x}=26.6. The sample standard deviation is also calculated: s=7.5s=7.5. You are tasked with determining a confidence interval using a given confidence level: γ=0.99\gamma=0.99.

To do this, you need to first determine t⋆t^\star such that P(|T|<t⋆)=0.99P(|T|<t^\star)=0.99. Then, the boundaries are determined by evaluating x‾±t⋆sn\bar{x}\pm \frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}.

(You can round t⋆t^\star to the nearest hundredth and the boundaries to the nearest tenth.)


  1. Determine the lower boundary of the confidence interval by using x‾−t⋆sn\bar{x}-\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}.
  2. Determine the upper boundary of the confidence interval by using x‾+t⋆sn\bar{x}+\frac{t^\star s}{\sqrt{n}}.
